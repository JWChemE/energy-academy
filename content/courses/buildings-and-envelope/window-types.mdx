Windows are the weakest thermal link in most facades. A wall might achieve 0.2 W/m2K while the glazing beside it struggles to reach 1.0. Understanding window performance is essential to controlling both heat loss and solar gain.

## Glazing generations

**Single glazing:** around 5.0 W/m2K. Effectively a sheet of cold radiating surface. Now rare in new work but common in older stock.

**Double glazing:** two panes with a sealed cavity. A basic air-filled unit reaches around 2.8 W/m2K; a modern argon-filled, low-E unit reaches 1.2-1.6 W/m2K.

**Triple glazing:** three panes, two cavities. Whole-window U-values of 0.8-1.0 W/m2K are achievable - approaching wall performance. Standard in cold climates and Passivhaus work.

## The whole-window U-value

A window's rating combines three parts:
- **Ug** - the centre-of-glass value (best)
- **Uf** - the frame value
- **Psi-g** - the edge-of-glass bridge at the spacer

The headline figure is **Uw**, the area-weighted whole-window value. A great glass unit in a poor frame still performs badly, so the frame matters as much as the glass.

## Frame materials

- **uPVC:** good insulation, low cost, multi-chambered profiles reach 1.2-1.4 W/m2K.
- **Timber:** naturally insulating, attractive, needs maintenance.
- **Aluminium:** strong and slim but highly conductive - must have a polyamide thermal break, or it becomes a giant thermal bridge.
- **Composite (timber-aluminium):** insulating timber inside, weatherproof aluminium outside. Premium option.

## Spacers and gas fills

The **warm-edge spacer** that separates panes used to be aluminium - a conductive bridge around the whole glass perimeter. Modern insulating spacers (stainless steel or polymer) cut edge losses and reduce condensation on the inner glass edge.

Cavities are filled with **argon** (or occasionally krypton for slim triple units) instead of air, reducing convective heat transfer across the gap by around 30%.

## Choosing well

For a heating-dominated UK climate, prioritise low Uw with a useful solar gain. For a cooling-dominated or heavily glazed building, the next lesson on coatings and solar control becomes the deciding factor.
