Modern glass is engineered with microscopically thin coatings that transform its thermal behaviour. The right coating can halve heat loss or block unwanted solar heat without darkening the view.

## Low-emissivity (low-E) coatings

A **low-E coating** is a transparent metallic-oxide layer that reflects long-wave (heat) radiation while letting visible light through. In winter it bounces indoor heat back into the room instead of letting it radiate out through the glass.

- **Soft-coat (sputtered) low-E:** highest performance, used inside sealed units. Brings centre-of-glass U-values down to 1.0-1.1 W/m2K.
- **Hard-coat (pyrolytic) low-E:** more durable, slightly lower performance, can be used on exposed surfaces.

Low-E is now standard in virtually all new double and triple glazing - it is the single biggest reason modern windows outperform old ones.

## Solar-control coatings

Where overheating is the concern, **solar-control coatings** reflect a portion of the near-infrared solar spectrum while keeping good visible-light transmission. This is the key to controlling cooling loads in glazed offices without resorting to dark tinted glass.

The trade-off is described by **light-to-solar-gain ratio** - the best solar-control glass admits plenty of daylight while rejecting much of the solar heat.

## Matching coating to climate and orientation

The correct specification depends on what dominates:

- **Heating-dominated building, north facade:** maximise low-E, accept or welcome solar gain.
- **South facade with overheating risk:** combine low-E with solar control, or rely on external shading.
- **Cooling-dominated office, west facade:** strong solar-control coating to block low-afternoon sun.

A single building often needs different glazing specs on different elevations. Specifying one product everywhere either wastes money or leaves a facade overheating.

## Coatings versus shading

Coatings work all year round and cannot be adjusted. External shading (covered next) can admit welcome winter sun and block unwanted summer sun. The best designs combine a sensible coating with adjustable or fixed shading tuned to each orientation.
