## EPC (Energy Performance Certificate)

Rates a building's estimated energy costs and carbon emissions on a scale A–G (A most efficient).

Required for all sales/lettings. Based on a standard methodology (SAP for residential, SBEM for commercial).

Informs: Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards (MEES), green mortgages, valuation.

## MEES (Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards)

Rented buildings must meet **EPC 'E' or better** or be delisted (cannot be let).

Applies to new tenancies and renewals from April 2020 (residential) and April 2023 (commercial).

Exemptions for hardship (cost of upgrade > 30% of valuation).

Impact: landlords must improve properties; tenants benefit from better-insulated, cheaper-to-heat homes.

## Building Regulations (energy standards)

New buildings and major renovations must meet fabric and system efficiency standards (U-values, boiler efficiency, insulation, controls).

Standards tightened periodically. By 2025, all new buildings will effectively be "zero-carbon ready" (near-zero emissions from a standard use case).

## Practical

If you own/manage a building: check its EPC, understand MEES obligations (if rental), and plan long-term improvements to meet evolving standards.
