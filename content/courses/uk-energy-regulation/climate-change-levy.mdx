The **Climate Change Levy (CCL)** is a tax on business use of energy to incentivise efficiency.

## What it covers

Electricity, natural gas, coal, fuel oil — all taxed. Renewables are exempt to encourage clean energy.

Typical rate: ~£0.03 per kWh for gas, ~£0.05 for electricity (varies).

## Exemptions & reductions

Organisations signed to a **Climate Change Agreement (CCA)** can get an 80% discount if they meet annual energy-reduction targets (typically 1–2.5% per year depending on the sector).

Renewable energy supplies are exempt.

## The CCA path

1. Meet the threshold (typically £3M+ annual business rates)
2. Commit to sector-specific energy targets
3. Monitor and report annually
4. Receive CCL discount (now ~0.39 pence/kWh gas, lower for electricity)

Many large energy users go through CCAs for the discount. The administrative burden is moderate (annual reporting) but the savings are substantial.

## Practical implication

CCL (+ CCA discount) costs most large organisations £100k–500k/year depending on consumption. Energy efficiency directly reduces this bill.
