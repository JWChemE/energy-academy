The **CRC Energy Efficiency Scheme** (now closed, ended 2019) was a mandatory carbon trading scheme.

Its successor is less direct, but **business electricity relief** (formerly an exemption from CCL) still applies in some contexts. Most focus now is on SECR and Carbon Reduction Commitment through financial accountability.

## Energy Company Obligation (ECO)

Energy suppliers are required to deliver efficiency measures to low-income and vulnerable households.

For business, the takeaway: ECO exists; residential energy suppliers are driven to improve their portfolio efficiency, which can create market opportunities for energy-service companies.

## Net effect

Business energy costs are driven by: electricity/gas rates (market), CCL (tax), CCA discount (if eligible), and SECR (reporting/accountability).

The compliance landscape has shifted from trading schemes to reporting (SECR) and tax incentives (CCL + CCA). Energy efficiency is financially rewarded.
