The UK's **carbon budgets** set legally binding CO₂ reduction targets for 5-year periods.

## The trajectory

- 5th carbon budget (2028–2032): 80% cuts vs 1990
- 6th carbon budget (2033–2037): 81% cuts vs 1990
- Path to net zero by 2050 (legally binding Climate Change Act)

## What it means

All sectors (power, transport, buildings, industry, agriculture) must collectively cut emissions to meet the budgets.

Buildings sector faces the biggest challenge: heating is largely fossil-fuel-based (gas boilers) and must electrify (heat pumps).

## Your responsibility

If you operate a large building or industrial facility, you'll be measured (via SECR). Energy efficiency + electrification + renewable generation are your levers to stay compliant.

Most organisations with a net-zero commitment are building a roadmap aligned to carbon budgets: 2030 checkpoint (~50% cuts), 2040 checkpoint (~75%), 2050 net zero.
