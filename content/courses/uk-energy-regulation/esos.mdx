**ESOS** (Energy Savings Opportunity Scheme) requires large organisations to audit energy regularly.

## Who must comply?

Large undertakings: ≥250 employees OR €50M turnover/€25M assets.

If you meet these, ESOS applies unless you're in a specific exemption (e.g., already certified ISO 50001 or Energy Audit Directive).

## What to audit?

All significant energy-using systems: buildings, industrial equipment, transport, cross-organisational systems.

## Timeline

Audits must be repeated every 4 years. Last deadline was Dec 2023; next is Dec 2027.

## What the audit must cover

- Technical and economic analysis of energy-saving potential
- Recommendations for improvement
- Quantified energy savings (estimated)

## Consequences

Non-compliance: fines up to £30,000 or 3% annual energy spend, whichever is greater.

Most large organisations hire an energy auditor accredited under ISO 17024 to deliver ESOS compliance.
