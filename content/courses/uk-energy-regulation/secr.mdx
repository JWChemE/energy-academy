**SECR** (Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting) requires certain organisations to disclose energy and carbon in their accounts.

## Who must report?

Large companies (≥500 employees) in the UK, or "non-UK undertakings" meeting the same threshold.

Listed on the London Stock Exchange? You must report.

## What to report?

- Total energy consumption (kWh) from all sources (electricity, gas, transport fuel, etc.)
- Estimated CO₂e emissions (Scope 1 + Scope 2; Scope 3 voluntary but increasingly expected)
- Energy intensity (kWh per employee or per £ turnover)
- Energy-saving measures implemented

## Format

In the Directors' Report (part of statutory accounts), or in a separate energy and carbon report.

## Deadline

4 months after financial year-end, as part of the statutory filing.

## Standards

Use:
- GHGP (Greenhouse Gas Protocol) for carbon accounting
- BEIS/DESNZ emission factors (updated annually)
- ISO 50001 if certified

Most organisations use a sustainability consultant or energy manager to prepare SECR.
