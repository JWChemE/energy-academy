UK energy and building policy is shifting fast.

## Key trends

1. **Heat pump mandates** — likely from 2025–2030, gas boilers will be banned from new buildings and major replacements
2. **Methane regulations** — methane leaks from gas networks being regulated
3. **Energy-from-waste constraints** — incinerators facing tighter rules (renewable waste preferred)
4. **Grid decarbonisation** — electricity increasingly wind + solar, lowering the carbon cost of electrification
5. **Hydrogen** — industrial heat, high-temp processes exploring hydrogen (not yet viable at scale)

## Net-zero roadmaps

Most sectors have published decarbonisation plans. Buildings roadmap: insulation + electrification (heat pumps) + renewable generation (solar/wind).

Timeline: 2030 (50% cuts), 2040 (75%), 2050 (net zero).

## Implications for you

If you operate a facility:
- Assume gas boilers won't be replicable past 2030 (plan heat-pump transition)
- Decarbonising electricity is happening automatically (grid); electrification of heating is your job
- Efficiency (insulation, controls, loss reduction) is the fastest payback
- Renewable generation (solar/wind) is now financially competitive with grid electricity in many cases

Long-term strategy: insulate → electrify → generate.
