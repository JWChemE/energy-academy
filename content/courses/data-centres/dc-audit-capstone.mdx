## How the stages work

This capstone follows the same staged format as the [Energy Audits course's own capstone](/courses/energy-audits/field-audit-capstone), because a data centre audit *is* an energy audit: this course has given you everything sector-specific on top of that general method:

1. **Plan & Scope**: choose the audit level, draw the boundary where the colocation contract draws it (facility systems, not customers' racks), and plan for security, live plant and change control before you walk in.
2. **On-Site**: the plant-room observations that matter in a facility that never sleeps, plus twelve months of intake and IT metering with one figure missing for you to derive.
3. **Analyse & Normalise**: build the facility model (fixed base, IT coefficient, weather term), compute an honest annual PUE, price each point of it, and catch the dilution effect that flatters the September number.
4. **Opportunities**: quantify the airflow programme and the UPS rebalancing from this course's diagnostics, then decide what belongs in the recommendations and what would breach the availability contracts that pay for everything.
5. **Report & M&V**: what the report owes its three audiences (board, CCA reporting, tender due diligence), and how savings get verified in a facility whose IT load grows year on year.

<Callout variant="key" title="The model is the honesty machine">
Stage 2's regression (base, IT coefficient, CDD term) is what separates this capstone's PUE from a brochure's: it prices each point of PUE at £25,200 a year, attributes any movement to genuine overhead reduction versus load-growth dilution, and gives the M&V stage an adjusted baseline that survives a growing denominator. Every stage after it is spending the credibility that model earns.
</Callout>

## What "audit-ready" looks like here

A strong run through this capstone means every stage's answers hold up on their own: the boundary defensible across the colocation contract, the hazards genuinely facility-specific (change control and live plant, not generic site safety), the model exact to the meter, the opportunities correctly separated from the SLA floors, and a PUE story that a due-diligence team could not embarrass.
