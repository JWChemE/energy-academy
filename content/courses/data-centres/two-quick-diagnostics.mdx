## What these two cases test

**Case 1** is the airflow spiral from the [previous lessons](/courses/data-centres/airflow-and-temperature), met in the wild: a legacy hall whose fans run flat out against their own bypass air, with the [cube law](/courses/motors-and-drives/why-vfds) waiting to pay out once someone fixes the air paths instead of adding more fans. **Case 2** is the [power chain's](/courses/data-centres/the-power-chain) signature waste: a healthy, contract-compliant UPS fleet parked at the worst point on its efficiency curve, where the fix is configuration, not equipment.

<Callout variant="key" title="Same method, data-centre numbers">
Both cases use only arithmetic you already have: the fan affinity law for the first, input = load ÷ efficiency for the second, each multiplied by the sector's relentless 8,760 hours. What they add is the judgement layer this sector demands: in both buildings the seductive wrong answer is *more equipment*, the right answer preserves every resilience commitment, and nothing proceeds except through change control.
</Callout>
