## Key takeaways

**HVAC is the biggest energy load in most buildings.** But it's also the most
controllable. The difference between a well-run and poorly-run system is often
10–30% of energy use — and much of that difference is free or very cheap to fix:

- **Weather compensation** (£3,000–5,000) saves 5–15% heating/cooling
- **Economizer** (£5,000–8,000) saves 30–50% cooling
- **Variable-speed drives** (£4,000–8,000) save 20–30% fan energy
- **Setpoint reset and scheduling** (free–£2,000) save 5–10%

A building that upgrades these four things often achieves 30–50% HVAC energy savings
for £20,000–25,000 total investment. Over 10 years, that's £30,000–50,000 in saved
fuel.

## Your next steps

<Callout variant="key" title="Three actions this month">
1. **Check setpoints** — are they higher in summer than winter? Can you lower them
   1–2 °C?
2. **Ask about economizer** — does your building have one? If yes, is it working? If
   no, is it a candidate for retrofit?
3. **Review controls** — does your BMS have weather compensation? If not, this is a
   quick win to propose.
</Callout>

waste energy — and what actually works to fix them.
