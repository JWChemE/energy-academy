When you walk into a building and want to audit the HVAC system, what do you look for?
Here's a practical checklist: the essentials you can assess without specialized
equipment.

## Basic inspection: the system type

1. **What type of system is it?**
   - **All-air** (ducts visible, central unit in plant room)
   - **Air-water** (small fan-coil units in zones, pipes in walls)
   - **Radiant** (ceiling panels, likely silent operation)
   - **Heat pump** (no boiler, outdoor unit on roof or ground)

2. **Age and condition:**
   - Unit visible: how old? Any rust, leaks, or damage?
   - Plant room: organized or chaotic? Pipes insulated or bare?

3. **Controls visible:**
   - Modern BMS (building management system) with sensors?
   - Manual thermostat on the wall?
   - Evidence of recent tuning/commissioning reports?

## The checklist: component by component

### Boiler (if present)
- Age? Efficiency rating (if known)?
- Flue-gas analysis on record? O₂ and CO readings?
- Blowdown setup: continuous or intermittent?

### Chiller (if present)
- Age? How many tons? Running at what % load?
- Evidence of fouling (capacity dropping)?
- Water treatment (logs visible)?

### Pumps and fan motors
- Variable-speed drives fitted? Or fixed-speed?
- Pipe insulation intact? Or bare hot pipes?
- Vibration or unusual noise?

### Ducts (all-air systems)
- Visible leaks or disconnections?
- Insulated? Or bare and losing heat?
- Cleanliness (dust accumulation)?

### Thermostats and sensors
- Where is the zone thermostat? In an occupied space, or in a corner/closet?
- Is it reading sunlight (east/west facing)?
- Multiple sensors, or just one for the whole building?

### Controls and setpoints
- What is the current heating setpoint? Cooling setpoint?
- Is there weather compensation? (Ask the operator or check the controller)
- Are there night setbacks programmed?
- Occupancy detection (motion sensors)?

## Operational clues: spotting inefficiency

| Observation | Implication |
| --- | --- |
| Building always 23 °C heating, 22 °C cooling (tight) | Over-controlled, likely wasting energy |
| No visible controls or thermostat | Building may be running wide-open, no modulation |
| Boiler running in summer (no cooling load) | Setpoint too high or system miscalibrated |
| Pump running full-speed (verify by checking valve position) | No variable-speed drive; likely 20–30% wasted energy |
| Multiple zones at different temperatures | System imbalanced; dampers stuck or miscalibrated |
| Fan noise audible | Ductwork full or dampers hunting; likely high pressure drop |

## Quick efficiency scoring

Rate your system 0–2 on each:

- **Control sophistication** (0 = manual; 1 = some automation; 2 = BMS, weather compensation)
- **Variable-speed drives** (0 = none; 1 = some; 2 = fans and pumps both variable)
- **Maintenance** (0 = no records; 1 = occasional; 2 = regular commissioning)
- **Part-load efficiency** (0 = oversized, running low load; 1 = OK; 2 = well-matched to load)
- **Age** (0 = >25 years; 1 = 15–25 years; 2 = under 15 years)

**Score 8–10:** modern, well-controlled system. Focus on fine-tuning.
**Score 5–7:** good candidates for controls retrofit (weather comp, variable drives).
**Score below 5:** major upgrade needed or equipment aging.

## Data collection for deeper audit

If you want to go deeper, collect:

- **Last 12 months of energy bills** (kWh/month) → see seasonal pattern
- **Setpoint history** (from BMS if available)
- **Equipment specifications** (make, model, date installed)
- **Maintenance records** (last service, any issues)
- **Thermal comfort complaints** (from occupants)

A more detailed **thermal audit** (~£3,000–5,000, takes 1–2 days) adds:
- **Spot temperature measurements** throughout the building
- **Infrared thermography** of walls/windows/ducts
- **Ductwork leakage test** (blower door)
- **System performance testing** (measure supply temps, flows, pressures)

This identifies specific efficiency losses and prioritizes retrofits.

<Callout variant="tip">
A 30-minute walk-through with a checklist often reveals quick wins. A deeper audit
(few hours + measurement) identifies the top 3–5 projects that will deliver real
savings.
</Callout>
