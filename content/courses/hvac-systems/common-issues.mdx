The same problems crop up in HVAC systems across buildings. Knowing the patterns —
and how they manifest — helps you diagnose and fix them quickly.

## Symptom: "The building is always too hot or cold"

**Root causes (in order of likelihood):**

1. **Sensor drift** — the thermostat is reading 2–3 °C off. Fix: recalibrate sensor
   (~£200). Takes 30 min.

2. **Stuck damper (VAV) or stuck valve (fan coil)** — the zone can't modulate its
   heating/cooling. Fix: clean or replace damper/valve (~£500–1,500). 

3. **Setpoint creep** — operators have raised the heating setpoint year-by-year, or
   cooling setpoint has drifted down. Fix: reset to design value (free, 30 min).

4. **Lack of weather compensation** — on mild days, the building is being heated as
   if it's winter. Fix: install weather compensation (~£3,000–5,000).

5. **No zoning** — one thermostat controls the whole building. One zone's need
   overrides others. Fix: add zone thermostats and controls (~£5,000–15,000).

Start with items 1–3 (cheap); move to 4–5 if those don't help.

## Symptom: "Heating energy is much higher than it should be"

**Likely causes:**

1. **No night setback** — building is being heated to 21 °C at night when 15 °C would
   suffice. Fix: enable night setback (free, 30 min).

2. **No weather compensation** — building is being heated at full capacity on mild
   days. Fix: install weather compensation (~£3,000–5,000).

3. **Uninsulated pipes** — hot water pipes running through unheated spaces lose heat
   continuously. Fix: pipe lagging (~£500–2,000 for a building).

4. **System imbalanced** — high-load zones are starving low-load zones of water,
   forcing higher supply temperatures. Fix: system balancing (~£2,000–5,000).

5. **Oversized boiler** — idling and cycling inefficiently. Fix: check actual load
   and consider replacing with right-sized unit (capital, but often justified).

## Symptom: "Cooling energy is much higher than it should be"

**Likely causes:**

1. **No economizer** — chiller running even on cool/mild days when outdoor air could
   cool. Fix: add outdoor-air economizer (~£5,000–8,000). **Typical savings:
   30–50% of cooling energy.**

2. **High cooling setpoint** — running at 20 °C when 22–23 °C is acceptable. Fix:
   raise setpoint (~0 cost, 5–10% savings).

3. **Internal loads not controlled** — office lighting running all day (even empty
   rooms), unused equipment consuming power and heat. Fix: occupancy sensors on
   lights, unplug unused devices (free/low cost).

4. **Fresh-air intake too high** — running 100% outdoor air year-round instead of
   demand-controlled. Fix: CO₂ sensors and damper reset (~£2,000–4,000).

5. **Chiller fouling** — water-side scaling or air-side corrosion reducing
   efficiency. Fix: water treatment and condenser cleaning (~£1,000–2,000).

## Symptom: "Different zones have very different temperatures"

**Root causes:**

1. **System imbalance** — water (or air) flow is unequal to zones. Some get too much,
   others too little. Fix: system balancing (~£2,000–5,000).

2. **Thermostat placement** — a sensor in a sunny or drafty location misrepresents
   zone temperature. Fix: sensor relocation or addition (~£500–1,000).

3. **Damper/valve leakage** — even when "closed," a damper or valve lets flow through,
   preventing full shutoff of that zone. Fix: replacement (~£500–1,500 per zone).

4. **Ductwork leakage (all-air systems)** — air bypasses closed dampers. Fix: duct
   sealing (~£1,000–3,000).

## Symptom: "Fan noise increased"

**Root causes:**

1. **Ductwork fouling** — dust buildup increases pressure drop, fan works harder. Fix:
   duct cleaning (~£500–2,000).

2. **Damper hunting** — VAV dampers oscillating (opening/closing rapidly) causes
   vibration. Fix: control tuning (~£500–1,000).

3. **Fan bearing wear** — mechanical wear increases vibration. Fix: bearing replacement
   or fan replacement (~£1,500–5,000).

4. **Air velocity too high** — oversized fan throttled down causes noise. Fix: review
   ductwork design (if recent retrofit, may require duct resizing, ~£5,000+).

## Symptom: "Condensation on windows / mould in bathrooms"

**Root causes:**

1. **High humidity** (winter) — indoor moisture too high for outside temperature. Fix:
   improve ventilation or install humidity control (~£2,000–5,000).

2. **Cold surfaces** (windows) — poor glazing (single-pane) or thermal bridging (cold
   frames). Fix: window replacement or frame insulation (~£5,000–20,000).

3. **No ventilation** — bathroom/kitchen exhaust off or blocked. Fix: check/clear
   vents and ensure exhaust fans run (~free to £500).

## A practical repair hierarchy

When HVAC is underperforming, fix in this order:

1. **Controls & commissioning** (free–£2,000): setpoints, tuning, sensor checking
2. **Easy retrofits** (£2,000–5,000): weather compensation, night setback, insulation
3. **System optimisation** (£3,000–10,000): economizer, variable drives, balancing
4. **Equipment upgrade** (£10,000+): chiller, boiler, major control system

Most buildings can achieve 10–20% energy savings from steps 1–3 before touching
equipment.

<Callout variant="tip">
When an HVAC system isn't working well, look for control problems first. Nine times
out of ten, the equipment is fine — it's just poorly tuned.
</Callout>
