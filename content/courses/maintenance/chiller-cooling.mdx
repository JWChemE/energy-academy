Chillers and cooling systems are major electrical loads, and their efficiency is highly sensitive to maintenance. A neglected chiller can lose a large fraction of its COP - and run up big electricity bills - while still apparently working.

## The dominant factor: condenser cleanliness

The single most important maintenance item for a chiller is **condenser cleanliness**. The condenser rejects heat; if it is fouled, the chiller must work against a higher condensing temperature, and efficiency falls sharply:
- **Air-cooled condensers:** dust and debris clog the fins, blocking airflow
- **Water-cooled condensers:** scale and fouling build on the tubes
- **Cooling towers:** fouled fill and poor water treatment raise condenser water temperature

Every degree of raised condensing temperature costs roughly 2-3% in chiller efficiency. Regular condenser cleaning is the highest-return chiller maintenance task.

## Refrigerant charge and circuit health

The refrigerant circuit must be correctly charged and leak-free:
- **Undercharge (often from leaks):** reduces cooling capacity and efficiency, and risks compressor damage
- **Overcharge:** raises condensing pressure and wastes energy
- **Superheat and subcooling:** checking these confirms correct charge and expansion-device operation

Refrigerant leaks also carry environmental penalties (many refrigerants are potent greenhouse gases), so leak detection and repair matter on two counts.

## Evaporator and air-side

- Keep **evaporator** surfaces clean for good heat transfer
- Replace **air filters** in air-handling systems (clogged filters raise fan energy and reduce airflow)
- Clean cooling coils

## Cooling towers (water-cooled systems)

- Maintain **water treatment** to prevent scale, corrosion, and biological growth (including Legionella control)
- Clean the **fill** to maintain heat rejection
- Check **fans, drift eliminators, and distribution** for proper operation

Lower condenser water temperature directly improves chiller COP, so tower maintenance pays back in chiller efficiency.

## Controls and operation

- Verify **chilled-water setpoints** are not lower than necessary (raising the setpoint a degree saves energy)
- Check **sequencing** runs the most efficient combination of chillers
- Enable **free cooling / economiser** operation in mild weather where available
- Confirm **pumps and variable-speed drives** modulate correctly

## Seasonal and routine maintenance

- **Regular:** monitor temperatures, pressures, and power; check for leaks and unusual noise
- **Pre-season:** service before the cooling season - clean condensers, check charge, test controls
- **Annual:** full service including compressor checks, oil analysis (where applicable), electrical inspection, and control recalibration

## The efficiency prize

Because chillers consume so much electricity, and because their efficiency degrades so readily with fouling and poor charge, chiller maintenance offers some of the largest energy savings in building services. A clean condenser, correct refrigerant charge, sensible setpoints, and a well-maintained cooling tower together can recover 10-20% or more of a neglected chiller's energy - far exceeding the maintenance cost.
