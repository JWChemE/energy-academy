Turning the principle of energy-focused maintenance into practice means knowing what to inspect, clean, and adjust on each major piece of plant. This lesson gives a practical, system-by-system checklist.

## Boilers and heating plant

- **Combustion tuning:** check flue-gas oxygen and CO; adjust the air-fuel ratio for efficient, complete combustion (each percentage point of excess air costs efficiency)
- **Flue-gas temperature:** rising temperature signals fouling - clean fireside surfaces
- **Scale and water treatment:** maintain treatment to prevent waterside scale; descale if needed
- **Controls:** verify setpoints, weather compensation, and sequencing
- **Insulation:** check boiler, pipe, and valve lagging is intact

## Chillers and cooling

- **Condenser cleaning:** wash fouled condenser coils - the single biggest chiller efficiency factor
- **Refrigerant charge:** check for correct charge and leaks; verify superheat and subcooling
- **Evaporator and filters:** keep heat-transfer surfaces and air filters clean
- **Cooling towers:** clean fill, maintain water treatment, check fan and drift

## Motors and drives

- **Bearings:** lubricate to schedule; listen and monitor for wear
- **Belts:** check tension and alignment; replace worn belts (slipping belts waste energy)
- **Cooling:** keep motor cooling fins and vents clear of dust (a hot motor is an inefficient one)
- **Alignment:** check coupling alignment to reduce drag and wear

## Compressed air

- **Leak survey:** ultrasonic survey and repair (leaks waste 20-30% of output)
- **Filters:** replace intake and line filters on schedule
- **Drains:** verify condensate drains work and do not leak air
- **Pressure:** confirm system pressure is no higher than needed

## Pumps and fans

- **Seals and bearings:** check for leaks and wear
- **Impellers:** inspect for fouling and damage
- **Filters and strainers:** keep clear to maintain flow at low energy

## HVAC and air handling

- **Air filters:** replace on schedule (clogged filters raise fan energy)
- **Coils:** clean heating and cooling coils
- **Dampers and actuators:** check they move freely and seal (stuck dampers waste energy)
- **Belts and bearings:** as for motors and fans

## Controls and sensors (all systems)

- **Sensor calibration:** check temperature, pressure, and flow sensors against references
- **Setpoints:** verify they have not drifted from the efficient design values
- **Schedules:** confirm time and occupancy schedules match actual use
- **Actuators:** check valves and dampers reach their commanded positions

## Building fabric and distribution

- **Insulation:** restore missing pipe, valve, and vessel lagging
- **Steam traps:** test and replace failed traps
- **Draught-proofing and seals:** maintain door seals and building air-tightness

## Using the checklist

Build these into scheduled maintenance rounds, with intervals matched to each item's rate of degradation and energy impact. Record readings (flue temperature, pressures, currents) each visit so trends reveal developing problems - the link to performance tracking later in this course. A consistent, energy-focused checklist keeps the whole estate near its design efficiency, capturing savings that no single project could match.
