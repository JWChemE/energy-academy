Turning the principle of energy-focused maintenance into practice means knowing what to inspect, clean and adjust on each major piece of plant. This lesson gives a practical, system-by-system checklist, drawn together from the detailed treatment each system gets in its own course, so that the [maintenance plan](/courses/maintenance/maintenance-plan) has concrete tasks to schedule. Each item reverses a specific efficiency loss.

## Boilers and heating plant

- **Combustion tuning**: check flue-gas oxygen and CO; adjust the air-fuel ratio (each point of excess air costs efficiency), covered in the [boiler-maintenance lesson](/courses/maintenance/boiler-maintenance).
- **Flue-gas temperature**: rising temperature signals fouling; clean fireside surfaces.
- **Scale and water treatment**: maintain treatment to prevent waterside scale.
- **Controls and insulation**: verify setpoints, weather compensation and sequencing; check that [lagging](/courses/insulation-systems/pipe-sizing) is intact.

## Chillers and cooling

- **Condenser cleaning**: the single biggest chiller efficiency factor, covered in the [chiller lesson](/courses/maintenance/chiller-cooling).
- **Refrigerant charge**: check charge and leaks; verify [superheat and subcooling](/courses/refrigeration-and-heat-pumps/superheat-subcooling).
- **Evaporator, filters and cooling towers**: keep heat-transfer surfaces clean; maintain tower water treatment.

## Motors, pumps, fans and compressed air

- **Bearings and belts**: lubricate to schedule; check belt tension and alignment (slipping belts waste energy), covered in the [motor lesson](/courses/maintenance/motor-pump).
- **Cooling**: keep motor fins and vents clear of dust (a hot motor is inefficient).
- **Compressed air**: [ultrasonic leak survey](/courses/compressed-air/leaks-detection) and repair (leaks waste 20 to 30% of output), filter changes, drain checks, and system pressure no higher than needed.

## HVAC, controls and fabric

- **Air filters and coils**: replace filters on schedule (clogged filters raise fan energy); clean coils.
- **Dampers and actuators**: check they move freely and seal (stuck dampers waste energy).
- **Sensors and setpoints**: [calibrate sensors](/courses/control-systems-and-bms/temperature-sensors) against references; verify setpoints and schedules have not drifted.
- **Fabric and distribution**: restore missing [insulation](/courses/insulation-systems/pipe-installation), test and replace failed [steam traps](/courses/steam-and-condensate/trap-failure), maintain door seals and airtightness.

<Callout variant="key" title="Record readings every visit, or the checklist only prevents breakdowns">
A checklist that only cleans and adjusts protects reliability, but it captures only half the value. The other half comes from *recording* the readings (flue-gas temperature, pressures, motor currents, specific energy) at every visit, because those readings are the data series that [performance tracking](/courses/maintenance/performance-tracking) needs to reveal developing problems and to prove that maintenance restored efficiency. Build the readings into every scheduled round, with intervals matched to each item's rate of degradation and energy impact. A consistent, energy-focused checklist that also logs its measurements keeps the whole estate near design efficiency and turns maintenance into a measurable discipline rather than a hopeful one.
</Callout>

The checklist covers scheduled tasks; the next lesson covers doing maintenance only when the equipment actually needs it, through condition monitoring.
