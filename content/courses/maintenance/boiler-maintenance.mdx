Boilers and fired heating plant are among the largest energy consumers in most facilities, and among the most sensitive to maintenance. Small lapses translate directly into wasted fuel.

## Combustion: the heart of efficiency

A boiler's efficiency depends first on good combustion. Maintenance keeps the air-fuel ratio right:
- **Too little air:** incomplete combustion, producing carbon monoxide and soot, wasting fuel and fouling surfaces
- **Too much air:** the excess air is heated and thrown up the flue, carrying energy away

Regular **combustion testing** measures flue-gas oxygen and CO, and the burner is tuned to the optimum - typically 3-5% excess oxygen. A boiler drifting to 8-10% oxygen can lose several percent efficiency. Annual (or more frequent) combustion tuning is one of the highest-return maintenance tasks available.

## Fireside fouling

On the combustion side, **soot** accumulates on heat-transfer surfaces, insulating them so heat goes up the flue instead of into the water. The telltale sign is rising **flue-gas temperature**: every 20 C rise represents roughly a 1% efficiency loss. Regular fireside cleaning (sweeping or brushing tubes) restores heat transfer.

## Waterside scale

On the water side, **scale** from hard water deposits on the tubes, with the same insulating effect. Even 1 mm of scale can add 5-8% to fuel use. Prevention through proper **water treatment** is far cheaper than cure:
- Maintain treatment chemicals and softening
- Control blowdown to manage dissolved solids without wasting heat
- Descale if deposits have built up

## Flue-gas heat recovery

Where fitted, **economisers** recover heat from flue gas to preheat feedwater. Keep their surfaces clean too - a fouled economiser stops recovering heat. On condensing boilers, ensure the return temperature stays low enough to keep them condensing.

## Controls and setpoints

Verify the control system is working as intended:
- **Weather compensation** adjusting flow temperature to outside conditions
- **Setpoints** not crept up unnecessarily
- **Sequencing** running the right number of boilers efficiently rather than several part-loaded
- **Burner modulation** tracking demand smoothly

## Insulation and distribution

A well-maintained boiler feeding poorly insulated pipes still wastes energy. Check that boiler casing, pipe, valve, and flange insulation is intact - restoring missing lagging is a quick win (a link to the insulation course).

## A boiler maintenance routine

- **Regular (weekly/monthly):** check flue temperature, pressures, water treatment, visible leaks; verify controls
- **Periodic (quarterly):** combustion testing and tuning, fireside inspection
- **Annual:** full service - clean fireside and waterside, service burner, test safety devices, recalibrate controls, inspect refractory and insulation

The reward is a boiler held near its design efficiency year-round. Given that fuel is the dominant lifetime cost of a boiler, this maintenance repays itself many times over in saved energy - while also ensuring safe, reliable operation.
