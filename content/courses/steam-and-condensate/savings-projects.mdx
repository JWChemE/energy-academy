Steam systems reward the methodical. The losses this course has walked through, flue and blowdown at the boiler, radiation in the mains, blowing traps, dumped condensate and vented flash, are all independently findable, priceable and fixable, and together they routinely add up to 15 to 30% of a site's steam fuel. This closing lesson assembles them into a sequenced programme, gathers the course's worked figures into one view, and sets the management routine that keeps the savings captured.

## The programme, in payback order

**Survey first, spend second.** A week of assessment work, the [efficiency assessment](/courses/steam-and-condensate/efficiency-assessment), an infrared [insulation survey](/courses/steam-and-condensate/steam-distribution), a full [trap survey](/courses/steam-and-condensate/trap-failure), and a [condensate mass balance](/courses/steam-and-condensate/condensate-return), produces the loss inventory that ranks everything else. If the site lacks steam metering, metering is item zero.

**Quick wins (paybacks in weeks to months).** Insulate the bare pipe and fittings the camera found; replace the failed traps; close the manual bypass valves the trap survey caught passing; institute the summer/weekend isolation regime the boiler log justifies.

**Systematic recoveries (typically under two years).** Automatic TDS control and blowdown heat recovery; condensate return extensions and pumping to the plant currently draining to gulley; a flash vessel feeding the hotwell; pressure review and reduction where processes allow, per the [pressure lesson](/courses/steam-and-condensate/steam-pressure).

**Capital measures (two to seven years).** Economisers on the flue, burner and control upgrades, and right-sized boiler replacement, all covered in the [boilers course](/courses/boilers-and-fired-systems); and decarbonisation options for the heat itself, which the [waste heat recovery](/courses/waste-heat-recovery) and [net zero](/courses/net-zero-roadmaps) courses take further.

## Keeping it: the steam KPIs

Steam savings decay without watch-keeping: traps keep failing, insulation gets stripped, bypasses creep open. Three numbers, trended monthly on the [M&T report](/courses/monitoring-and-targeting/dashboards), hold the line: **kWh of fuel per tonne of steam** (the master efficiency figure), **condensate return percentage**, and **trap failure rate** from the rolling survey. Each has an owner, a target, and an [exception threshold](/courses/monitoring-and-targeting/exception-reporting); together they make backsliding visible within a month instead of at the next audit.

<Callout variant="key" title="Steam is the most improvable system most sites own">
Few utilities combine steam's unit cost (≈£48/tonne at reference prices), its typical state of neglect, and the maturity of its fixes. Every remedy in this course is standard, catalogued engineering with decades of practice behind it; none is experimental. The scarce ingredient is simply someone who has surveyed the system, priced the losses, and owns the three KPIs. That someone is now you.
</Callout>
