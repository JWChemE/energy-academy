Once you've assessed the system, prioritise improvements by payback period.

## Common projects and savings

**Trap replacement program** (all failed traps + preventive replacement every 5–7 years):
- Cost: 500–1,500 GBP per trap, 10–15% of traps per year
- Savings: 300–1,000 GBP per trap annually
- Payback: 1–5 years

**Pipe insulation** (add or upgrade on uninsulated/poorly insulated lines):
- Cost: 30–100 GBP per m for labour + material
- Savings: 10–50 GBP per m per year (depends on pipe size and use)
- Payback: under 1–3 years

**Flash recovery** (install flash tank and low-pressure line):
- Cost: 5,000–15,000 GBP
- Savings: 20,000–50,000 GBP/year (10–15% of steam cost)
- Payback: 1–2 years

**Condensate return system** (if none exists, install polished return + booster pump):
- Cost: 20,000–50,000 GBP
- Savings: 100,000–300,000 GBP/year (40–50% of boiler fuel for feedwater heating)
- Payback: under 1 year (often justifies itself in months)

**Boiler replacement** (old, oversized boiler → modern, right-sized):
- Cost: 50,000–200,000 GBP
- Savings: 30,000–100,000 GBP/year (efficiency + right-sizing)
- Payback: 2–7 years

## Implementation sequence

1. Quick wins first (trap replacement, obvious insulation)
2. Medium-term (flash recovery, condensate polishing)
3. Long-term (boiler replacement, system redesign)

This sequence builds confidence and funds, and often earlier projects reduce later costs.
