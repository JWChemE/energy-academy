A **steam system audit** checks three main areas: boiler efficiency, steam quality/losses, and condensate recovery.

## Boiler efficiency

Measure or estimate:
- **Fuel input** (gas flow, oil consumption)
- **Steam output** (calculated from condensate return + process steam, or steam flow meter)
- **Efficiency** = (energy in steam) / (energy in fuel)

Typical boiler efficiency: 80–85% for fire-tube, 85–90% for water-tube.

Compare to nameplate (what the boiler is rated for). If actual is much lower than nameplate, the boiler may be oversized, fouled, or poorly tuned.

## Steam distribution losses

Walk the steam line and look for:
- **Uninsulated or poorly insulated pipes** (feel for heat, or use infrared)
- **Leaks** (hissing, visible steam)
- **Pressure drop** (check inlet vs outlet pressure; excessive drop suggests undersized pipes or fouling)

Estimate heat loss: small line (25 mm diameter, 100 m uninsulated, 10 bar steam, 20°C ambient) loses ~50 kW continuously. Serious waste.

Cost to insulate: ~£1,000 for that line. Savings: ~30 kW × 24 h × 365 = ~250 MWh/year ≈ £20,000–30,000 at typical steam cost. Payback: weeks.

## Steam quality & traps

- **Test condensate** from the return line downstream of traps. If steam is present (condensate boils/flashes), traps are failing.
- **Count and check traps** with infrared. A facility with 50 traps should have no more than 5–10% visibly failing.
- **Listen** for hissing (stuck open), banging (stuck closed), or sluggish response.

## Condensate recovery

- **Is condensate being returned?** Measure if return line is active.
- **What temp is it?** Should be 80–90°C. If it's below 60°C, it's losing heat to the environment.
- **Is there a flash tank?** If not, flash steam is wasted. Adding one is a quick win.

## Rough payback estimates

- Insulating uninsulated pipes: payback often under 1 year
- Replacing failed traps: payback 1–3 years
- Installing flash recovery: payback 2–5 years
- Boiler tuning/cleaning: payback under 1 year (but requires a technician)
- Replacing an oversized boiler with a smaller, modern one: payback 3–7 years
