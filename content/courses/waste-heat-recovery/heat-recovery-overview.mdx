**Why recover waste heat?** Because it's "free" energy you're currently throwing away. A typical industrial facility loses 20-30% of fuel input as waste heat.

## Business case

**Boiler flue gas example:**
- Fuel input: 1,000 kW
- Boiler efficiency: 85%
- Waste heat in flue: 150 kW (at 180°C)
- Recoverable heat (condensing boiler conversion): 30 kW (latent heat)
- Energy cost (assume £0.05/kWh): 30 kW × 8,760 h × £0.05 = £13,140/year
- Project cost (condenser retrofit): £20,000
- **Payback: 1.5 years**

## Typical payback timescales

- **Flash tank recovery** (steam condensate): 1–3 years
- **Boiler flue condensing retrofit**: 1.5–4 years
- **Chiller condenser heat recovery**: 2–5 years
- **Heat exchanger on process exhaust**: 3–8 years
- **Ground-source heat pump** (using shallow ground as heat source): 8–15 years (long, but GSHP is very efficient)

Fast payback projects (under 3 years) are almost always worth doing.

## Challenge: Size and integration

Waste heat equipment takes up space. A heat exchanger or recovery unit needs fitting into tight mechanical rooms. Integration with existing systems (plumbing, electrical, controls) adds cost.

**Key insight:** Even modest waste heat recovery (recovering 10-20% of the waste stream) often has acceptable payback if the sink is close by.
