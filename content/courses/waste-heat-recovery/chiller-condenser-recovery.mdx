A **chiller produces two temperatures:** cold water for cooling and hot condenser water rejected to the atmosphere (usually wasted).

## The opportunity

Chiller: 10 MW cooling capacity with 3 MW compressor work = 13 MW heat rejected at the condenser (50–60°C).

Standard practice: Cool this to ~35°C in a cooling tower, release to air.

Better: Use the 50–60°C condenser water for domestic hot water, space heating (in mild seasons), or pool heating.

**Cost:** Plumbing, heat exchanger, controls. ~£20,000–50,000.
**Savings:** 3 MW × 8,760 h × £0.08/kWh = £2,112,000/year (if fully utilised). Realistically, 30–50% utilisation = £600,000–1,000,000/year.
**Payback: < 1 year** (for large chillers) to 2–3 years (for smaller systems).

## Challenge: Load timing

Chiller runs summer (peak cooling). DHW demand may be year-round but lower in summer. Utilisation determines payback.

**Solution 1:** Use condenser heat for daytime DHW (office building, peak occupancy), cooling tower for evening/overnight.

**Solution 2:** Add thermal storage (hot tank) to absorb excess condenser heat for later use.

**Best case:** Simultaneous cooling demand (chiller) and heating demand (DHW, reheating coil on cool days) = maximum utilisation.
