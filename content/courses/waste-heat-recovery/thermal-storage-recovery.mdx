Three technologies capture and redistribute waste heat:

## Thermal wheels (rotating heat exchangers)

A rotating, porous core (silica gel or ceramic) absorbs heat from exhaust air on one side, releases it to supply air on the other as it rotates.

Pros: High effectiveness (80-90%), compact, simultaneous cooling/heating possible.
Cons: Some cross-contamination of odours/particles, requires rotating mechanism.

Common in: HVAC systems with high ventilation loads (offices, hospitals).

## Heat pipes

A sealed tube containing refrigerant. Heat at one end vaporises it; condensation at the other releases heat. Gravity and capillary action return liquid to the hot end.

Pros: No moving parts, passive (no power), very reliable.
Cons: Only works in one orientation, limited to medium temperature differences.

Common in: Industrial process heat recovery, exhaust stacks.

## Flash tanks (condensate recovery)

When high-pressure steam/hot condensate passes through a pressure-reducing valve, some flash to lower-pressure steam.

Example: 10 bar condensate flashing to 1 bar releases ~10% of flow as steam, which is captured and used for low-pressure heating.

Pros: Simple, high payback.
Cons: Requires steam/condensate system to exist.

Common in: Industrial plants with steam systems (refineries, food processing, breweries).
