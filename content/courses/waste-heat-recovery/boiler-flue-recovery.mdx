**Condensing boiler retrofit** or **condensing boiler replacement** is one of the fastest-payback waste heat projects.

## How it works

Standard boiler: flue gas ~200°C, mostly sensible heat. Some heat is lost to the stack.

Condensing boiler: flue gas is cooled until water vapour in the gas condenses, releasing latent heat (~2,250 kJ/kg). Flue gas leaves at 50–60°C instead of 180°C.

Energy recovered: ~10–15% of fuel input (most commonly 10%).

## Economics

Fuel input: 100,000 kWh/year (85% efficiency = 85,000 kWh useful heat).
Latent recovery: 10% of 100,000 = 10,000 kWh/year.
Cost: £0.05/kWh × 10,000 = **£500/year savings**.

Condensing boiler retrofit cost: £3,000–8,000.
**Payback: 6–16 years.**

Payback is longer than flash tanks or chiller recovery, but condensing is sometimes worth it for longevity and carbon reduction.

## Installation requirements

- Flue gas condensation produces acidic condensate (carbonic acid from CO₂); must drain to floor drain
- Boiler and piping should be stainless steel or acid-resistant
- Feedwater return should be hot enough to avoid condensation on boiler surfaces (typically >40°C)

## Limitations

- Works best in buildings where return water is already moderately warm (district heating loop, high base load)
- Not ideal if return water is very cold (risk of internal boiler condensation, corrosion)
