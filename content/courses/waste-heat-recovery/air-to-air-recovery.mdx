**Air-to-air heat recovery** (ERV / HRV) recovers energy from exhaust ventilation air, reducing the energy needed for incoming fresh air.

## HRV vs ERV

**HRV (heat recovery ventilator):** Recovers sensible heat (temperature) only. Good for dry climates.

**ERV (energy recovery ventilator):** Recovers sensible AND latent heat (humidity). Better for humid climates where dehumidification is costly.

In both, exhaust air (usually warm/moist in winter) passes through a core, warming/humidifying incoming fresh air without mixing.

## Typical performance

- ERV/HRV sensible effectiveness: 70–85%
- Latent effectiveness (ERV only): 60–75%

If exhaust is 22°C and outdoor is 0°C, an 80% effective HRV pre-heats incoming to ~18°C (saving heating energy).

## Applications

**Office buildings:** Fresh air requirement (ASHRAE 62.1: 10 CFM/person) in winter waste energy. HRV recovers it.

**Residential:** Tight homes require balanced ventilation. ERV recovers heat and humidity.

**Datacenters:** Cold outdoor air in winter goes to waste. Recovery reduces cooling demand.

## Maintenance

Air-side fouling (dust, pollen) is common. Clean filters monthly, core annually (or chemical wash every 3-5 years).

**Cost:** £3,000–10,000 installed. Savings: 10–20% of heating/cooling energy (depends on climate and ventilation rate).
