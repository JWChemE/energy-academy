Industrial and commercial processes generate enormous waste heat: foundries, breweries, food processing, laundries, etc.

## Types of process waste heat

**Exhaust air:** Ovens, dryers, furnaces. Temps: 100–400°C. Can heat water, DHW, or pre-condition incoming air.

**Cooling water:** Compressors, reactors, machinery. Temps: 30–60°C. Lower value, but easy to recover.

**Steam condensate:** Return from process steamer. High value if captured at high temperature; lost if cooled to ambient.

## Retrofits

**Example: Bakery with 180°C oven exhaust**

Recover heat to preheat incoming air (combustion air for the oven or fresh air for the space).

Cost: £15,000–30,000 (air-to-air heat exchanger, ductwork, controls).
Savings: 100 kW average, 8,000 h/year = 800 MWh/year × £0.05 = **£40,000/year**.
**Payback: < 1 year.**

**Example: Brewery with warm water cooling loop**

Recover 50°C condenser water for cleaning hot water (normally heated by steam or boiler).

Cost: £10,000–20,000.
Savings: 50 kW average = 400 MWh/year × £0.05 = **£20,000/year**.
**Payback: < 1 year.**

## Key variables

1. **Temperature:** Higher = more valuable
2. **Continuity:** 24/7 processes > intermittent
3. **Load proximity:** Adjacent buildings = lower piping cost
4. **Timing match:** Thermal load must align with supply

Industrial plants with continuous processes often find waste heat recovery projects with payback under 2 years.
