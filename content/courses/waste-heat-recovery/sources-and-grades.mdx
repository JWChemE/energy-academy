**Waste heat** is thermal energy that would otherwise be lost. Not all waste heat is equally valuable — it depends on temperature, availability, and whether there's a nearby thermal load.

## Common waste heat sources

**High-temperature (150–350°C):**
- Boiler flue gas
- Industrial furnace exhaust
- Incinerator stack

**Medium-temperature (50–150°C):**
- Chiller condenser water
- Process steam condensate
- Air compressor discharge

**Low-temperature (20–50°C):**
- Refrigeration system condenser
- Building exhaust air (ventilation)
- Wastewater
- Equipment cooling water

## Grading heat by value

**High-grade heat:** High temperature, large quantity, stable supply. Example: 200°C boiler flue, continuous generation.

**Medium-grade heat:** Moderate temperature (60–100°C), decent quantity, intermittent. Example: chiller condenser, daytime only.

**Low-grade heat:** Low temperature, small quantity, variable. Example: warm exhaust air in mild weather.

Value depends on **finding a thermal sink** (a process or space that needs heating) at the same time.

## Matching supply to demand

The best waste heat project has:
1. **Supply:** Continuous or predictable waste heat generation
2. **Demand:** Nearby thermal load at compatible temperature
3. **Timing match:** Supply and demand aligned (not supply in summer, demand in winter)

Example of poor match: foundry furnace heat in summer, heating need in winter only.

Example of good match: Chiller running summer, space cooling + condenser heat recovery for DHW (same season, same building).
