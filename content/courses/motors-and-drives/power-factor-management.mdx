**Power factor** (PF) is the fraction of apparent power that does useful work. Low PF wastes energy and costs money.

## Real, reactive, and apparent power

- **Real power (P, kW):** does useful work (heating, motion)
- **Reactive power (Q, kVAr):** oscillates, wasted in inductors (motors, transformers)
- **Apparent power (S, kVA):** total = √(P² + Q²)
- **Power factor** = P / S = cos(φ), where φ is the phase angle

At PF = 1.0 (resistive), all power is real. At PF = 0.7 (inductive motors), 30% is reactive (wasted).

## Why poor PF costs money

1. **Larger cables and switchgear needed** for the same real power
2. **I² R losses in wiring** scale with total current (higher at poor PF)
3. **Utility charges:** Most commercial tariffs charge for kVA, not just kW
   - 100 kW motor at PF 0.85: draws 100 / 0.85 = 118 kVA
   - Same motor at PF 0.95: draws 105 kVA
   - Difference = 13 kVA, billed as higher demand charge

**Example:** If demand is 500 kVA, improving from PF 0.85 to 0.95 reduces it to 474 kVA, saving demand charge on 26 kVA.

## Causes of low PF

- **Induction motors** — inherently inductive, especially when:
  - Oversized (running light)
  - Lightly loaded (20–50% of capacity)
- **Transformers** — also inductive
- **Ballasts in old fluorescent lights** — reactive
- **VFDs and other power electronics** — non-linear, create harmonics

## Improving PF

**Method 1: Right-size motors**
- An oversized motor at 50% load has poor PF
- Replacing with a right-sized motor improves PF to 0.92+

**Method 2: Capacitor banks**
- Capacitors inject reactive power, opposing inductors
- Sized to raise PF to 0.95 or better
- Cost: 2,000–5,000 GBP for typical facility
- Savings: 500–1,500 GBP/year
- Payback: 3–5 years

**Method 3: Active harmonic filters**
- More expensive, but also reduce harmonics
- Good if equipment with VFDs is causing poor PF and harmonics

**Method 4: Load better** — if motors are lightly loaded, increase load (or right-size the motor).

## Checking your facility

Check the electricity bill:
- If it shows PF, look at it monthly
- If PF < 0.95, you're paying for reactive power
- Target: PF > 0.95

Use a power-factor meter (handheld clamp-on: £200–500) to check PF in motor rooms, HVAC plants, etc.
