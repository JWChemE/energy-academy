## What these two cases test

**Case 1** is the sector's signature heat-recovery pairing from the [previous lesson](/courses/food-manufacturing/heat-recovery-in-food), met in the wild: washdown water heated by gas every night while the ammonia plant rejects heat to the roof all day, and a desuperheater waiting to bridge them. **Case 2** is the defrost double payment from [running the cold plant](/courses/food-drink-foundations/running-the-cold-plant): a fixed timer set "to be safe" years ago, costing the site twice for every excess hour, at the heaters and again at the compressor.

<Callout variant="key" title="Same method, food-factory numbers">
Both cases use only arithmetic you already have: mass × specific heat × ΔT for the water, and heat ÷ COP for the removal penalty. What they add is the judgement layer this sector demands: neither building needs new plant, both fixes must respect the hygiene and safety floors, and on a real site "install a bigger boiler" is always on offer when the actual answer is a heat exchanger or a controls change.
</Callout>
