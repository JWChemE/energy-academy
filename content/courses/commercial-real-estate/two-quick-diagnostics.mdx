## What these two cases test

**Case 1** is the out-of-hours base load from the [previous lesson](/courses/commercial-real-estate/out-of-hours-and-bms), met in the wild: half-hourly data showing a building that never sleeps, and a number to put on it before you name the cause. **Case 2** is the dead-band failure that same lesson warned about: an air-handling unit heating and cooling the same air because years of setpoint nudges left the heating target above the cooling trigger, the control fault the [BMS course](/courses/control-systems-and-bms/control-strategies) covers mechanically, here priced in pounds.

<Callout variant="key" title="Same method, office-specific numbers">
Both cases use only arithmetic you already have: demand × hours × price for the first, coil duty through boiler efficiency and chiller COP for the second. What the cases add is judgement under realistic distractors, because on a real site "install more plant" is always on offer, and both of these buildings need less running, not more machine. If the [out-of-hours lesson](/courses/commercial-real-estate/out-of-hours-and-bms) made sense, these should feel like direct applications.
</Callout>
