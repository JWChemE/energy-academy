How you fund a project affects its attractiveness and true cost.

## Cash purchase

**Pros:**
- No interest cost
- Simple, no paperwork
- Own the equipment outright

**Cons:**
- Large upfront capital outlay
- Opportunity cost (that money could have been invested elsewhere)
- No leverage (can't do multiple projects)

**TCO:** Full cost upfront.

## Bank loan

**Typical terms:** 5–10 year term, 4–8% interest

Example: 50k GBP at 6% over 5 years = ~966 GBP/month
- Total paid: 966 × 60 = 57,960 GBP
- Interest cost: 7,960 GBP
- Effective cost: higher than cash purchase, but you can do other projects in parallel

**Good if:** IRR > loan rate and you have other worthy projects.

## Lease

**Typical terms:** 5–10 year lease, 100–150% of equipment cost

Advantage: Predictable monthly cost, no maintenance surprise, technology refresh at lease end.

Disadvantage: No ownership, leases are usually 20–50% more expensive than buying.

**Good if:** You want flexibility and predictability, or equipment becomes obsolete quickly.

## Performance contract (ESCO)

**How it works:**
1. Energy Service Company (ESCO) finances and installs improvements
2. You pay from the energy savings (usually 50–70% of savings go to ESCO, 30–50% to you)
3. ESCO guarantees a minimum savings level; if not met, they pay the difference

**Pros:**
- No upfront capital
- Guaranteed savings (risk on ESCO)
- ESCO handles design and commissioning

**Cons:**
- More expensive than cash purchase or loan (ESCO takes a cut)
- Longer payback (you get only 30–50% of savings)
- Contracts can be complex, locking you in for 10–15 years

**Good if:**
- You lack upfront capital
- You want to de-risk the project
- Project is complex and needs professional guarantees

## Comparing methods

**Cash: 50k cost → 50k NPV at 5% discount rate**

**Loan (5 years, 6%): Effective cost 57.96k, but spread over 5 years**
- Each year: 11,592 GBP (loan + interest)
- If savings exceed this and continue post-loan, net benefit over 15 years is still positive

**ESCO (you get 40% of 5k/year savings): 2k/year to you**
- ESCO gets 3k/year
- 15-year cost: ESCO collects 45k, you pay 30k, but saved 75k in energy
- Net to you: -30k (paid) + 75k (saved) = +45k

Choose based on: available capital, risk tolerance, project complexity, and long-term strategy.
