Real-world examples where life-cycle thinking changes decisions.

## Example 1: LED vs halogen lighting

| | Halogen | LED |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Upfront cost | 5 GBP | 50 GBP |
| Power | 60 W | 10 W |
| Lifespan | 2,000 hours (~3 years) | 50,000 hours (~15 years) |
| Annual energy cost (8 h/day) | 175 GBP | 29 GBP |

**15-year TCO (8 h/day operation):**
- Halogen: 5×5 (replacements) + 175×15 = 2,625 + 2,625 = **5,250 GBP**
- LED: 50 + 29×15 = 50 + 435 = **485 GBP**

LED wins dramatically. Simple payback: (50 − 5) / (175 − 29) = 45 / 146 = 0.31 years (3.7 months). TCO shows LED saves 90% over 15 years.

## Example 2: Variable-speed pump vs fixed + valve

**Fixed pump with damper:**
- Capital: 5k GBP (pump + damper)
- Average load: 50% (damper closes half the time, wasting power)
- Annual energy (100 kW at 50% avg load): 50 kW × 8,760 h = 438 MWh × £0.05/kWh = **21,900 GBP/year**

**Variable-speed pump (VFD):**
- Capital: 15k GBP (pump + VFD)
- Same average load: 50%, but speed drops, so power ∝ flow³
- Annual energy: ~6.25 kW × 8,760 h = 54.75 MWh × £0.05/kWh = **2,738 GBP/year**
- Maintenance (VFD): 200 GBP/year

**15-year TCO:**
- Fixed: 5k + 21,900×15 = **333.5k**
- VFD: 15k + (2,738 + 200)×15 = **46,170**

**VFD wins by £287k!** Payback: (15k − 5k) / (21,900 − 2,938) = 10k / 18,962 = 0.53 years.

## Key lesson

When operating cost dominates (as in energy projects), high upfront cost for efficiency is almost always justified. TCO, not simple payback, tells the truth.
