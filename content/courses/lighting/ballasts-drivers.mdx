Lamps rarely connect straight to mains power. Between the supply and the light sits control gear - a ballast for fluorescent, a driver for LED - and its efficiency and quality matter more than people realise.

## Why control gear exists

A fluorescent tube needs a high striking voltage and then current limiting, or it would draw ever-increasing current and destroy itself. An LED needs a stable, regulated DC current quite unlike mains AC. The ballast or driver provides this conditioning.

## Fluorescent ballasts

- **Magnetic (wire-wound) ballasts:** the old technology. They work but waste 10-20% of the lamp's power as heat in the ballast itself, and cause flicker.
- **Electronic ballasts:** lighter, more efficient, run lamps at high frequency to eliminate visible flicker, and enable dimming. Replacing magnetic with electronic ballasts is itself an energy measure.

Ballast losses are real energy: a magnetic ballast on a 58 W tube might draw an extra 10 W continuously.

## LED drivers

The **LED driver** converts mains AC to the regulated low-voltage DC the LEDs need. Driver quality determines much of an LED product's real performance:

- **Efficiency:** good drivers are 85-92% efficient; poor ones waste more and run hot.
- **Lifetime:** the driver, not the LED, is usually the first thing to fail - cheap electrolytic capacitors cooked by heat. Driver quality is why two outwardly identical LED fittings can have very different real lifespans.
- **Dimming capability:** drivers must be compatible with the dimming protocol (mains dimming, 0-10 V, DALI) for controls to work.
- **Power factor and harmonics:** quality drivers maintain high power factor and low harmonic distortion, avoiding penalties and supply problems across many fittings.

## Power factor and the wider system

Large numbers of cheap drivers with poor power factor draw extra apparent current and inject harmonics into the building's electrical system, which can cause losses and equipment problems elsewhere - a link back to the power-quality issues in the electrical-systems courses. Specifying gear with power factor above 0.9 and low harmonic distortion protects the whole installation.

## The takeaway

When specifying lighting, look past the lamp to the control gear. Electronic ballasts over magnetic; quality LED drivers with good efficiency, high power factor, suitable dimming, and proven thermal design. The driver is where a cheap LED fitting reveals its true cost - in wasted watts and early failure.
