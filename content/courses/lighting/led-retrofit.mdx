Replacing old lamps with LED is one of the most reliable energy projects available - mature technology, large savings, and paybacks often under three years. But doing it well takes more than buying the cheapest bulbs.

## The savings case

LED draws 50-80% less power than the technology it replaces while lasting several times longer:

- Halogen downlight (50 W) to LED (6 W): 88% saving
- Linear fluorescent (58 W tube) to LED (22 W): 60% saving plus ballast losses removed
- Metal-halide high-bay (400 W) to LED (150 W): 60%+ saving

A typical office retrofit cuts lighting energy by 50-65%, with the longer LED life slashing maintenance and relamping costs on top.

## Payback drivers

The economics improve with:
- **Long operating hours** (warehouses, 24/7 areas pay back fastest)
- **High lamp wattage** being replaced (high-bay, floodlighting)
- **High access cost** for relamping (high ceilings, where LED's long life avoids costly access)
- **Adding controls** at the same time, compounding the saving

Paybacks range from under a year for high-hour industrial lighting to 3-5 years for low-hour spaces.

## Lamp replacement versus luminaire replacement

- **Retrofit lamps ("tubes"/"corn" lamps):** swap the lamp, keep the fitting. Cheapest and quickest, but you inherit old, possibly inefficient optics and must address the existing ballast (bypass or compatible).
- **New LED luminaires:** replace the whole fitting. More cost and disruption, but better optics, integrated drivers, controls-ready, and a clean warranty. Usually the better long-term choice for major refurbishments.

## Quality pitfalls

Cheap LED disappoints in ways that are easy to miss at purchase:
- **Poor lifespan** from inadequate driver and thermal design (it runs hot and fails early)
- **Bad colour rendering or wrong colour temperature** making spaces look grim
- **Flicker** causing discomfort and headaches
- **Overstated lumen claims** delivering less light than promised
- **Glare** from bare, undiffused LEDs

Specify reputable products with verified lumen output, CRI above 80, low flicker, suitable colour temperature, and a meaningful warranty (5 years+). Independent schemes and photometric data help separate good products from bad.

## Don't just relamp - redesign

A retrofit is a chance to do better than like-for-like. LED's efficiency and directionality often mean fewer fittings can do the job; daylight and occupancy controls can be added; light levels can be corrected where the old scheme over- or under-lit. Treating the retrofit as a mini redesign, rather than a straight swap, captures the full saving.
