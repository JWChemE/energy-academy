The lamp (or "light source") is where electricity becomes light. The technology chosen sets the efficiency, lifespan, light quality, and running cost of the whole installation.

## Incandescent and halogen

The original electric light: a filament heated until it glows. Cheap to buy, excellent colour rendering, but desperately inefficient - 90% of the energy becomes heat, not light. Halogen is a marginally better variant. Both are now largely phased out for general lighting.

- Efficacy: 10-25 lm/W
- Life: 1,000-2,000 hours

## Fluorescent

An electric discharge through mercury vapour produces UV, which a phosphor coating converts to visible light. For decades the commercial workhorse (T8 and slimmer, more efficient T5 tubes).

- Efficacy: 50-100 lm/W
- Life: 10,000-20,000 hours
- Drawbacks: contains mercury, needs a ballast, output dips in the cold, finite switching cycles

## LED

A light-emitting diode converts electricity directly to light in a semiconductor. Now the default for virtually all new and retrofit lighting.

- Efficacy: 100-180 lm/W
- Life: 30,000-50,000+ hours
- Strengths: instant start, dimmable, directional, robust, no mercury, excellent at low temperatures
- Considerations: quality varies widely; driver and thermal design determine real-world life

## Light quality dimensions

Choosing a lamp is not just efficacy and life:

- **Colour temperature (kelvin):** warm white (2,700-3,000 K) for hospitality and homes; neutral (4,000 K) for offices; cool (5,000-6,500 K) for industrial and task areas.
- **Colour rendering index (CRI):** how faithfully colours appear under the light, from 0-100. Offices want CRI above 80; retail, galleries, and clinical settings 90+.
- **Glare and distribution:** how the light is delivered affects comfort as much as quantity.

## Why LED wins

LED dominates because it leads on nearly every axis at once - efficacy, life, controllability, switching, and (increasingly) colour quality. The remaining skill is specifying *good* LED products and integrating them with controls, rather than choosing the technology at all.
