Leaks are the biggest waste in compressed air systems and often go undetected because they're silent.

## How much a leak costs

A 1 mm hole at 7 bar loses ~10 CFM (~0.3 m³/min) continuously.

Energy cost: If a 50 kW compressor produces 200 CFM (typical), a 10 CFM leak is 5% of output = 2.5 kW × 24h × 365d × £0.10/kWh = £2,190/year for one small leak.

A facility with 20 similar leaks wastes ~£44,000/year.

## Detection methods

**Listening:** Works in quiet areas only. Many leaks are too subtle to hear.

**Ultrasonic detector:** Handheld device (~£800-2,000) "listens" to the ultrasonic hiss of escaping air (30+ kHz). Finds leaks instantly in noisy environments.

**Soapy water test:** Apply soapy water to suspected spots. Bubbles indicate a leak. Slow but free.

**Dye test:** Add dye tracer to the air; leaks show colored spray. Professional method.

## Repair ROI

Sealing a 5% leak costs ~£500-2,000 (technician + parts). Saves ~£11,000/year. **Payback: < 1 month.**

Annual leak audit (with ultrasonic detector) is standard best practice. Cost: £1,000-2,000. Savings: typically £20,000-50,000.
