Compressed air offers some of the most reliable energy savings in any facility. The improvements below are ranked roughly by payback, fastest first.

## Quick wins (payback under 1 year)

**Fix leaks.** Leaks typically waste 20-30% of compressor output. A 3 mm hole at 7 bar leaks air worth around 1,500 GBP/year. An ultrasonic survey plus a tagging-and-repair programme is the single highest-return project available.

**Lower the system pressure.** Every 1 bar reduction cuts compressor energy by about 7%. Many systems run at 7-8 bar when 6 bar would serve every tool. Find the genuine minimum and set there, with a small margin.

**Switch off at night and weekends.** An idling compressor still consumes 20-40% of full-load power. A simple timer or interlock eliminates this. Check for "phantom" weekend running first - it is surprisingly common.

## Medium-term projects (1-3 years)

**Fit a variable-speed drive (VSD).** Where demand varies, a VSD compressor matches output to load instead of cycling load/unload. Savings of 15-30% are typical on fluctuating systems.

**Sequence multiple compressors.** A central controller runs the most efficient combination of machines for the current demand, avoiding several part-loaded units. Savings of 5-15%.

**Recover the heat.** Reuse the 80-90% of input energy that becomes heat for space or water heating.

## Building the case

Sub-meter the compressor house so savings are verifiable. Establish a baseline of specific energy (kWh per m3 of free air delivered), implement changes one at a time, and measure against the baseline. This both proves the saving and protects it from drifting back.

A combined leak-repair, pressure-reduction, and control programme commonly cuts a site's compressed-air energy by 30-50% with an average payback well under two years.
