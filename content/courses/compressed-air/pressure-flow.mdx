Compressed air is delivered in terms of **bar** (pressure) and **CFM** (flow rate). Both must be specified to understand the energy requirement.

## Pressure and system energy cost

Higher pressure = more work required. Compressing to 8 bar vs 6 bar is proportionally harder.

Rule: Every 1 bar of excess pressure wastes ~10% energy.

If a facility runs at 8 bar but only needs 6 bar, reducing to 6 bar saves ~20% of compressor energy.

**System pressure should match the highest downstream requirement**, no higher.

## Flow (CFM) and demand

**CFM** = cubic feet per minute of air. A pneumatic tool might use 5-10 CFM; multiple tools running simultaneously demand 50+ CFM.

Peak demand = sum of simultaneous tool use (not sum of all possible tools).

Oversizing the compressor (to handle hypothetical peak) wastes money. Right-size to **actual peak demand**, with a buffer for growth.

## Pressure drop and efficiency penalty

Air loses pressure as it travels through pipes. **Every 0.1 bar drop costs ~1% energy.**

Pipe diameter, length, and debris (moisture, oil, dust) cause drop. A 100m run of 0.5" pipe might drop 2 bar (20% energy waste). Upsizing to 1.5" pipe drops it to 0.5 bar (5% waste).

Solution: Larger pipes, strategic drains, and filters.
