System pressure must be controlled to prevent excess energy waste and equipment damage.

## How pressure is regulated

A compressor discharge valve opens/closes to maintain setpoint (e.g., 7 bar). As demand falls, the valve modulates or the compressor enters "load-unload" mode (compressor runs unloaded, wasting energy).

**Load-unload control:** Compressor runs at full power but supplies no pressure (energy wasted). Typical on reciprocating compressors.

**Variable-displacement (modulating):** Compressor output adjusts smoothly to match demand. Typical on screw compressors. More efficient.

## Pressure drop in the system

System pressure = compressor discharge pressure minus pressure drop in pipes/hoses. If drop is 1 bar, you need 8 bar discharge to get 7 bar at the tool.

Solution: Run at the minimum discharge pressure needed to overcome pipe drop, then maintain that setpoint (usually 6-8 bar for most facilities).

## Reducing system pressure

If tools only need 5 bar but system is set to 8 bar, reducing setpoint saves ~30% energy. Test tools first to verify they work at lower pressure.

Cost to adjust: None. Savings: 10-30% of compressor energy for each 2-bar reduction.
