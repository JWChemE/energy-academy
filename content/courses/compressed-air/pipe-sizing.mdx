Undersized pipes waste energy through pressure drop. Oversized pipes cost more upfront. Optimization is key.

## Pipe sizing rule

Pressure drop should not exceed 0.5 bar over the supply line (from compressor to first use point). For longer runs, 1 bar drop is acceptable.

Velocity of air in pipes should be 4-6 m/s (good balance of cost and efficiency). Higher velocity increases drop.

## Drying compressed air

Air is compressed with moisture in it. Compressed air at 40°C and high pressure can hold water, which condenses and damages tools.

**Refrigerated dryer:** Cools air to 3°C (dew point -5°C). Lower cost, lower maintenance. Typical: £3,000-10,000.

**Desiccant dryer:** Uses absorbent material to reach -40°C dew point. Higher energy (regeneration heats the dryer), higher cost. Typical: £10,000-20,000.

Choice: Use refrigerated unless extreme dry air is needed (electronics manufacturing, etc.).

## Filtration

Air must pass through filters before use:
- Intake filter (dirt, pollen)
- After-cooler filter (moisture, oil)
- Point-of-use filters (dust, debris)

Clogged filters increase pressure drop. Replace per maintenance schedule (~500-1000 hours of run).
