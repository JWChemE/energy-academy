**COP** (coefficient of performance) is the key efficiency metric for heat pumps and refrigeration.

## Definition

**COP** = useful output (heating or cooling) / compressor work input

- COP = 3 means 3 kW of heating for 1 kW of compressor work
- COP = 4 is better (4 kW for 1 kW work)
- COP varies with temperature conditions (outdoor temp for heat pump, chilled water temp for chiller)

## SCOP and seasonal variations

**SCOP** (seasonal COP) = average COP over a heating or cooling season.

Actual COP varies throughout the season:
- Mild days: COP is high (small temperature lift, easy work)
- Cold days: COP is low (large lift, hard work)
- Peak heating/cooling demand: COP is typically worst (hardest working)

Example: Air-source heat pump SCOP 3 means average COP across the winter is 3, but on the coldest day it might drop to 2.

## Comparison to electric heating

Electric resistance heater: COP = 1 (1 kW heat per 1 kW electrical input).

Heat pump COP = 3 is 3× more efficient (same heating with 1/3 the electricity).

**Cost comparison:** 1 MWh heating from:
- Electric heat (COP 1): 1 MWh electricity × £0.15/kWh = £150
- Heat pump (COP 3): 0.33 MWh electricity × £0.15/kWh = £50

Heat pump saves £100/MWh.

## Improving COP

1. **Lower temperature lift:** If heat pump heats to 45°C instead of 60°C (lower supply), COP improves ~5% per °C drop.
2. **Higher source temperature:** For air-source, milder outdoor temps = higher COP. For ground-source, stable ground temp = consistently high COP.
3. **Right-sizing load:** Oversized heat pump at part-load has poor COP. Match size to typical demand.
