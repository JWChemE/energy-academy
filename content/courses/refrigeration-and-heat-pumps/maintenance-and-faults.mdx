Common refrigeration system faults and how to diagnose:

## Refrigerant leaks

**Symptom:** Cooling capacity drops, high superheat, high discharge pressure.

**Cause:** Pinhole corrosion, vibration fatigue, damage during installation.

**Fix:** Use refrigerant sniffer (electronic sensor), locate leak, repair/replace piping, recharge.

**Prevention:** Annual pressure check, leak-sealing oil additives.

## Superheat/subcooling errors

**Symptom:** Temperature control poor, compressor overheating (high superheat).

**Cause:** Expansion device (TXV) stuck or misadjusted, refrigerant charge wrong.

**Fix:** Measure superheat/subcooling, adjust expansion device, recharge if needed.

## Oil circulation problems

Compressor needs oil for lubrication. If oil gets trapped in evaporator/condenser, bearing starves.

**Symptom:** Compressor overheating, noise, eventual failure.

**Fix:** Oil return line installer error. Ensure return line has proper slope, oil traps in place.

## Sensor drift

Thermostat or pressure sensor reads wrong = control is incorrect.

**Symptom:** Setpoint never reached, erratic cycling, unnecessary auxiliary heating.

**Fix:** Recalibrate sensor against reference, or replace if drifted beyond tolerance.

## Scale/fouling

Hard water in condenser loop deposits scale, reducing heat transfer.

**Symptom:** Condenser pressure high, efficiency drops.

**Fix:** Chemical descaling, water treatment (inhibitors, softening).

## Electrical issues

Contactor stuck, wiring loose, capacitor failed = compressor won't start.

**Symptom:** No cooling, compressor hums but doesn't turn.

**Fix:** Check electrical supply, capacitor, motor winding (megohm test).

Most require a service technician. Budget £500–1,500 for major repairs.
