Most refrigeration and heat pump systems don't run at full design capacity all the time. **Part-load performance** is critical for seasonal efficiency.

## Capacity control methods

**On-off cycling:** Compressor runs full-speed until demand is met, then stops. Resumes when demand rises. Simple but inefficient at low load (cycling losses).

**Unloading valve (solenoid discharge):** High-pressure gas bypasses the evaporator back to suction. Compressor runs but no cooling. Wastes ~30% energy compared to stopping.

**Variable-capacity compressor:** Adjusts displacement (twin-screw, scroll with slides) to match load. Good efficiency down to 25–50% capacity.

**Variable-frequency drive (VFD):** Slows compressor motor to match load. Excellent efficiency (COP improves at part-load if condenser water is cooler).

## Part-load COP example

Full-load (100%): COP = 3.5

At part-load:
- 75% load with on-off cycling: COP ≈ 3.0 (cycling losses)
- 75% load with VFD: COP ≈ 3.6 (compressor slows, superheat improves, cubing law works in reverse)
- 25% load with on-off cycling: COP ≈ 2.0 (frequent cycling, inefficient)
- 25% load with VFD: COP ≈ 3.2 (compressor runs at low speed, quite efficient)

**Seasonal impact:** Systems with VFD can deliver 15–25% better seasonal COP vs on-off cycling.

## Condenser water temp

For chiller: If condenser water is cooler (e.g., 26°C vs 32°C), COP improves ~2–3% per °C.

Cool condenser water from cooling tower on mild days = better COP.

Waterside economiser: On cold days, bypass chiller entirely and use cooling tower water directly.
