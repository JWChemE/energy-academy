**Water-cooled systems** use water (chilled or hot) as the heat transfer medium instead of air.

## Chiller (cooling)

Evaporator cools chilled water (5–7°C typical). Condenser rejects heat to condenser water loop (usually 28–35°C).

Water is pumped to fan coils, radiators, or process cooling throughout the building.

Advantages: Quieter than air-cooled (no outdoor fan noise), more efficient (condenser water can be cooler), flexible distribution.

Disadvantages: Requires water treatment (corrosion inhibitor, biocide), more complex plumbing.

## Heat pump water heater

Evaporator (outdoor) absorbs heat from air or ground. Condenser heats domestic hot water (DHW) to 50–60°C.

Used for DHW only, or integrated with space heating via water loops.

Efficiency: COP 2–4 typical, saving 50–60% energy vs electric immersion heater.

## Cooling tower

Condenser water from chiller is cooled in a cooling tower (evaporative cooler: sprays water, fan draws air through, evaporation cools the water).

Outdoor temp achievable: ~3–5°C above wet bulb (humidity-dependent). In dry climates, very efficient.

Maintenance: Water treatment, regular cleaning to prevent algae and scale.

## Ground-source heat pump (GSHP)

Uses ground as heat source/sink instead of air.

Loop in ground (borehole, horizontal, or pond) maintains ~10–15°C year-round.

Advantages: Stable temperature = consistent COP (3.5–4.5 heating/cooling), works in any climate.

Disadvantages: High installation cost (borehole £2,000–5,000+), long payback (8–15 years typically).

Good for: New construction, long building life, high operating hours, space/noise constraints.
