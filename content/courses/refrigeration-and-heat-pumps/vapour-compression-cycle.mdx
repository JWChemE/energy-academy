All refrigeration and heat pump systems use the **vapour-compression cycle** — a closed loop pumping heat from cold to hot using a compressor.

## The four components

**1. Compressor:** Raises pressure (and temperature) of low-pressure vapour. Input: mechanical work (electricity). Output: high-pressure hot gas.

**2. Condenser:** High-pressure hot gas is cooled and condenses to liquid. Heat is rejected (to air, water, or ground).

**3. Expansion device:** High-pressure liquid is expanded to low pressure. Temperature drops dramatically (throttling).

**4. Evaporator:** Low-pressure liquid evaporates (boils) in response to cold load. Absorbs heat from the space being cooled.

Cycle repeats continuously.

## Example: refrigerator

- **Evaporator** (inside, cold): Refrigerant boils at -20°C, absorbing heat from the fridge, cooling it to 4°C.
- **Compressor** (outside): Raises pressure, temp to 60°C.
- **Condenser** (outside, warm): Rejects heat to room air. Refrigerant condenses to liquid at 60°C.
- **Expansion device:** Throttles liquid back to low pressure.
- Cycle repeats.

Net result: Heat moved from inside (4°C) to outside (room air), using electricity to drive the compressor.

## Energy balance

Compressor work + heat absorbed = heat rejected

Example: Chiller with 100 kW cooling capacity, 30 kW compressor work:
- Heat absorbed from chilled water: 100 kW
- Work input: 30 kW
- Total heat rejected to condenser water: 130 kW

Efficiency metric: COP (coefficient of performance) = 100 / 30 = 3.33
