Practical steps to improve refrigeration/heat pump efficiency:

## Refrigerant charge optimization

Measure superheat and subcooling. Adjust charge (add/remove refrigerant) to achieve:
- Superheat 5–8°C
- Subcooling 5–10°C

Proper charge can improve COP by 5–10%.

Cost: Service technician visit, £200–500.
Savings: ~1–2% annual energy reduction on average.

## Cleaning condenser/evaporator

Heat exchanger fouling (scale, dust) reduces effectiveness.

Condenser cleaning: High-pressure water, chemical descaling.
Cost: £500–2,000.
Benefit: 3–8% efficiency recovery.

Schedule: Annual in dirty environments, every 2–3 years in clean.

## Sensor calibration

Check thermostats, pressure transducers. Miscalibrated sensors cause control drift.

Cost: £200–500.
Benefit: Prevents part-load inefficiency, ensures proper defrost cycling (heat pump).

## Setpoint optimization

Heating: Lower setpoint by 1°C = ~3% energy saving (less heat needed). Trade-off: comfort.

Cooling: Raise setpoint by 1°C = ~3% saving. Trade-off: comfort.

Condenser setpoint: Lower by 1°C (water-cooled chiller) = ~2% COP improvement. Cost: larger cooling tower (capital expense).

## Maintenance

Regular servicing (annually or per OEM): Replace filters, check for leaks, confirm oil circulation, verify electrical connections.

Cost: £500–1,000/year.
Benefit: Prevents gradual degradation, extends equipment life.
