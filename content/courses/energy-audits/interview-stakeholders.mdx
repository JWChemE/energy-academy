Talking to people who use and operate the building reveals patterns, problems, and constraints you won't see on a walk-through.

## Who to interview

- **Facilities manager/engineer**: maintenance history, known issues, past attempts to save energy
- **Operations/occupancy**: usage patterns, hours of operation, future plans (expansion, downsizing, changes)
- **Finance/energy manager**: budget, energy costs, past projects, priorities
- **Complaints**: if there's a comfort issue (too cold in winter, too warm in summer), investigate

## Key questions

**Operations:**
- What are typical hours of operation? Is the building occupied weekends, holidays?
- Are there variations (shift work, seasonal, event-driven)?
- Have occupants complained (too cold, too hot, stuffy, draft)?

**Maintenance:**
- What's broken or failing right now?
- When was the last major service on heating/cooling/lighting?
- Any equipment failures in the past 5 years?
- What's the maintenance routine (reactive only, or preventive)?

**Energy & costs:**
- What's the annual energy bill? How does it vary by season?
- Any recent changes (renovations, new equipment, occupancy)?
- What are the biggest concerns (cost, comfort, carbon)?

**Past projects:**
- Has anything been done to save energy? What worked? What didn't?
- Were any projects abandoned? Why?

**Future plans:**
- Any planned renovations, expansions, or equipment replacement?
- Is there interest in renewable energy, electrification, or net-zero targets?

## Red flags to ask about

- "The building is always cold in winter" → poor insulation, low setpoints, or heating undersized
- "Air conditioning is running all night" → controls aren't working, occupancy scheduling is wrong
- "We've had boiler failures" → oversizing, poor maintenance, or age
- "Lights are always on" → occupancy sensors missing or broken

## Document it

Write up the interview notes. Quote direct observations ("radiators are hot even though it's mild outside", "the thermostat is at the far end of the building, not where people work").
