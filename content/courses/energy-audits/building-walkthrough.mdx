A **building walk-through** is a systematic visual inspection to understand how energy is used and identify obvious inefficiencies.

## Before you arrive

- Obtain building plans (floor layout, major equipment)
- Collect utility bills (last 12 months)
- List any known issues (draft complaints, equipment failures, renovation plans)
- Schedule access with facilities/operations

## What to look for

**Envelope:**
- Condition of windows (single, double, triple glazing; seals intact?)
- Insulation visibility in attics, crawlspaces (present, adequate thickness?)
- Air leakage points (gaps around doors, penetrations, cracks)
- External shading (trees, adjacent buildings, blinds)

**Heating & cooling:**
- Age and condition of boilers, chillers, heat pumps
- Heat exchanger fouling (is it regularly cleaned?)
- Pipe insulation (present? sufficient thickness?)
- Control valves and actuators (visibly functional?)
- Thermostat type and location (single for whole building? In occupied zone?)

**HVAC:**
- Filters (clean or clogged?)
- Fan operation (running continuously or on schedule?)
- Ductwork insulation and sealing
- VAV boxes and dampers (responsive?)
- Ventilation dampers (are they open/closed as appropriate?)

**Lighting:**
- Lamp type (incandescent, halogen, fluorescent, LED?)
- Occupancy sensors (present? functional?)
- Daylighting (are blinds/curtains blocking windows?)
- Lights on in unoccupied areas?

**Controls & systems:**
- BMS or basic thermostats?
- Does the system match the occupation pattern? (office hours? 24/7?)
- Setpoints (are they reasonable? 21°C in winter, 22°C in summer?)
- Scheduling (does the system respond to weekends, holidays?)

**Maintenance:**
- Maintenance logs available and current?
- Equipment condition (rust, dirt, leaks?)
- Last service dates (is equipment overdue?)

**Occupancy & operation:**
- Talk to facilities staff: What's the biggest complaint? When do systems run? Any failures? What's been tried before?
- Ask about future plans (refurbishment, expansion, downsizing)

## Documentation

Take photos of equipment nameplates, controls, and visible issues. Record thermostat setpoints, equipment ages, and observations.

## Output

A list of potential improvements (rough, not yet quantified) and areas needing deeper investigation.
