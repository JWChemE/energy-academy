Once you have your baseline, you can identify inefficiencies and rank them by payback.

## Analysis approach

Compare to benchmarks: look up similar buildings' energy intensity (kWh/m²/year). If yours is much higher, inefficiency is obvious.

Plot consumption vs. weather/occupancy. Does the slope match expected? Steep slope suggests poor insulation or oversized heating.

If you have sub-meters, what's using the most? Compare to standards (lighting should be 10–15 W/m²; if yours is 30, upgrades are obvious).

Combine visual inspection findings (uninsulated pipes, failed traps, oversized boilers, poor controls) with measurement data.

## Quantifying savings

For each opportunity, estimate:

**Energy saved (kWh/year):**
- Comparison method: "If we reduce HVAC run hours by 2 h/day, we save 2 h × 20 kW × 365 = 14,600 kWh/year"
- Engineering: "Boiler efficiency 80% → 90% on 100,000 kWh/year input = 12,500 kWh/year saved"
- Supplier data: "LED retrofit claimed to save 50% on lighting (currently 50 kW) = 25 kW × 8,760 h × 50%"

**Cost saved (£/year)** = kWh saved × £/kWh rate. Electricity ~0.25 GBP/kWh, gas ~0.05 GBP/kWh equivalent.

**Project cost** — Material + labour. Get supplier quotes.

**Payback (years)** = cost / annual savings. Target: less than 3–5 years.

**CO₂ saved (tonnes/year)** = kWh saved × 0.2 kg CO₂/kWh (UK grid average).

## Ranking examples

- Boiler cleaning: 5,000 kWh/year saved, 500 GBP cost, 0.4-year payback
- LED retrofit: 25,000 kWh/year, 50,000 GBP cost, 8-year payback
- Weather compensation: 15,000 kWh/year, 8,000 GBP cost, 2.1-year payback
- Trap replacement: 20,000 kWh/year, 10,000 GBP cost, 2-year payback
- Insulation: 10,000 kWh/year, 40,000 GBP cost, 16-year payback

Prioritise by payback. A 0.4-year payback (boiler cleaning) pays for itself in 5 months — a clear quick win.
