Everything an audit gathers converges here: the walk-through's suspicions, the loggers' evidence, the interviews' context and the baseline's reference points become a list of specific, costed, ranked opportunities. This is the intellectual centre of the audit and the part clients actually pay for. The craft has two halves: estimating each measure's saving honestly, and ranking the portfolio so the client knows exactly what to do first.

## Three ways to size a saving

Nearly every estimate uses one of three methods, in descending order of confidence.

**Measured comparison** takes the logged data and applies the change arithmetically: the AHU logging showed 5,640 excess hours at 11 kW, so scheduling saves 62,040 kWh. Where the audit measured well, this method is close to unarguable.

**Engineering calculation** models the physics with stated assumptions: a boiler at 78% efficiency replaced by one at 92% cuts fuel in the ratio of efficiencies; an insulation measure is sized from surface areas and temperature differences, as the [insulation course](/courses/insulation-systems) shows. State every assumption, because the reviewer will test them.

**Vendor or literature figures** ("VSDs typically save 15 to 30% on variable loads") are the weakest tier and belong only where measurement was impossible, always at the conservative end of the published range, always attributed. An audit whose big numbers all rest on vendor claims is a brochure.

Whatever the method, savings compound with prices and carbon: kWh saved × tariff = £ saved; kWh × the [government conversion factor](/courses/uk-energy-regulation/secr) = tCO₂e saved. Use the site's actual marginal prices, reliefs included, per the [regulation course](/courses/uk-energy-regulation/business-electricity-relief).

## Ranking the portfolio

Simple payback (cost ÷ annual saving) is the audit's working currency because everyone understands it, and the [economic analysis course](/courses/economic-analysis) adds NPV and IRR for the larger measures. The standard presentation groups measures into three tiers:

<WorkedExample title="a ranked shortlist">
  <Given>
    Three measures from a site's audit:
    - Heating controls and scheduling: £2,000 cost, £3,000/yr saving
    - VSD on the main extract fan: £15,000 cost, £12,000/yr saving
    - LED lighting replacement: £24,000 cost, £8,000/yr saving
  </Given>
  <Find>The paybacks and the recommended order.</Find>
  <Solution>
    Controls: 2,000 ÷ 3,000 = **0.7 years**. VSD: 15,000 ÷ 12,000 = **1.25 years**.
    LED: 24,000 ÷ 8,000 = **3.0 years**.

    Recommended order follows payback, and the sequencing carries a message worth making
    explicit to the client: the controls measure funds itself within the financial year,
    and its savings then part-fund the VSD, whose savings part-fund the LEDs. A ranked
    list is not just an ordering; it is a self-financing programme.
  </Solution>
</WorkedExample>

Two refinements keep the ranking honest. **Interactions**: measures overlap, so a heating-control saving shrinks once insulation reduces the heating load; estimate the combined programme, not the sum of isolated measures, or state that the figures assume independent implementation. **Risk**: attach a confidence note to each figure (measured / calculated / literature), so the client can see which numbers are load-bearing.

<Callout variant="key" title="Underpromise: the audit's numbers will be checked">
An audit's reputation is set eighteen months later, when someone compares the bills against the promises. Estimate conservatively: take the cautious end of ranges, degrade vendor claims, and round savings down and costs up. A report that promised £30,000 and delivered £35,000 makes the next project easy to fund. One that promised £50,000 and delivered £35,000 poisons the well, despite identical engineering.
</Callout>

## Do not discard the non-energy benefits

Many measures carry value beyond the meter: maintenance savings from LED lifetimes, comfort and productivity from controls, capacity freed by [power factor correction](/courses/electrical-science/power-factor), compliance value under [MEES](/courses/uk-energy-regulation/epc-and-building-standards). List them qualitatively alongside the energy numbers; boards frequently fund on the combination.

The list is built. The final lesson covers the document and the meeting that get it implemented.
