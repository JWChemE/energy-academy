When you walk up to a boiler plant, how do you quickly assess whether it's a problem?
Age is not the whole story — a well-maintained 25-year-old boiler can outperform a
neglected 5-year-old one. Here's what to look for.

## The big picture: age, size, and load

**How old is the boiler?**
- Pre-2005: almost certainly under 85% efficient, likely 75–80%
- 2005–2015: ~88–92% depending on type
- Post-2015: likely 92%+ (possibly condensing at 95%+)

**Is it oversized?**
- Ask: what's the design heat loss of the building/load? Compare to boiler capacity.
- If the boiler is 1.5–2× the design load, it's oversized and will cycle inefficiently.

**What is typical load?**
- Talk to the operator or check a year of bills. Is the plant running continuously,
  or does it turn on/off frequently?
- Frequent cycling = inefficiency. A plant running 50% of the time at 30% of capacity
  is badly tuned.

<Callout variant="key" title="A boiler's efficiency is not just about the machine — it's about the job it's being asked to do">
An efficient boiler running at 10% load in oversized plant wastes more energy than a
less-efficient boiler right-sized to the demand.
</Callout>

## The burner: combustion quality

**What does it look like?**
- **Blue flame** = clean combustion. Good sign.
- **Yellow/orange flame** = incomplete combustion or dirty burner. Energy is being
  wasted.

**Flue-gas analysis** (if you have/hire a portable analyser):
- **O₂ 3–5%**: good (efficient excess air)
- **O₂ 8–12%**: too much excess air, losing efficiency
- **CO below 50 ppm**: good. **>100 ppm**: incomplete combustion, waste

- **Flue temperature 140–160 °C** (non-condensing): normal. **>180 °C**: fouling or
  excess air drift
- **Flue temperature 45–55 °C** (condensing): good (condensing is happening)

If you see high O₂, high CO, or high flue temperature, the burner needs tuning or
cleaning.

## The boiler exterior

**Soot, cracks, or leaks?**
- Cracks in the casing = loss of insulation; water will eventually seep out
- Weeping joints = potential corrosion inside
- Heavy soot around air intake = combustion air is dirty (inlet filter needs
  replacing)

**Insulation jacket intact?**
- Many older boilers are bare steel with missing insulation blankets. Radiation loss
  can be 3–5% of output. If visible, it's easy to fix.

## Maintenance history

**Is there a maintenance log?**
- A boiler with annual burner cleaning and servicing is worth more than one with ad-hoc
  repairs.
- Ask: when was it last cleaned? If >2 years ago, it's probably overdue.

**Has the system been water-treated?**
- No chemical treatment history = scale and corrosion risk
- Corrosion inside = reduced heat transfer = lower efficiency

## The distribution system

**Is the system balanced?**
- Visit two radiators (or check zone temperatures). Do they feel similar, or is one
  hot and another cold?
- Unbalanced = the boiler is running hotter than it needs to, wasting energy

**Are pipes insulated?**
- Hot pipes in unheated spaces = wasted heat. Easy to fix with pipe lagging.

## Red flags: likely to need action

| Finding | Implication | Typical action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Boiler >25 years old, under 85% efficient | High fuel consumption, poor load response | Replacement consideration |
| O₂ >8%, CO >100 ppm | Burner drift, incomplete combustion | Tuning, cleaning, possible oxygen trim retrofit |
| Flue temp >180 °C | Fouling or poor combustion | Cleaning, tuning |
| No blowdown control or >3% continuous blowdown on hard water | Wasted heat | Water softening or blowdown heat recovery |
| Setpoint at 75–80 °C year-round | No weather compensation | Install weather compensation or manual reset by season |
| System unbalanced (some radiators cold) | Running boiler hotter than needed | System balancing |

## Quick assessment score

Rate the boiler 0–2 on each of these:

- **Burner tune** (0 = dirty/yellow flame; 1 = acceptable; 2 = excellent O₂/CO)
- **Efficiency** (0 = unknown or under 80%; 1 = ~88%; 2 = >92% or condensing)
- **Age & condition** (0 = >20 yrs or visibly worn; 1 = 10–20 yrs; 2 = under 10 yrs or
  recently serviced)
- **Controls** (0 = fixed setpoint, no weather comp; 1 = some controls; 2 = weather
  compensation)
- **Maintenance** (0 = no history; 1 = occasional; 2 = regular annual service)

**Score 8–10**: well-maintained, minor tuning possible. Plan replacement in 5–10 years.

**Score 5–7**: good opportunity for optimization (tuning, controls, system balancing).
May extend life 3–5 years.

**Score below 5**: replacement or major retrofit likely needed within 1–2 years.

## Commissioning a boiler assessment

If you're serious about a boiler plant, a **professional thermal efficiency audit**
(£1,500–3,000) is worth the investment:
- Flue-gas analysis at multiple loads
- System operating point analysis
- Water treatment assessment
- Report with prioritised recommendations

The test pays for itself if it uncovers even one major issue (e.g., "your blowdown
is oversized" = £5,000/year waste).
