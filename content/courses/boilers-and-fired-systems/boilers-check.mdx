## What comes next

Boilers are almost never the only heat source in a plant. Steam systems, HVAC, and
waste heat recovery all matter. In the next course, **HVAC Systems**, we'll look at
how buildings are heated and cooled: the load that the boiler must meet.

If your site has steam (common in older industrial plants), the Level 2 course
**Steam & Condensate Systems** covers traps, condensate return, and distribution
losses.

In the meantime, here are the practical actions you should take if you manage a boiler:

<Callout variant="key" title="Three things to check this month">
1. **Get a flue-gas analysis**: measure O₂, CO, and flue temperature. Cost: ~£200
   for a contractor, or ~£1,500 for a portable analyser if you do many.
2. **Check the boiler setpoint and weather compensation**: is it set for the
   current season? Is weather compensation enabled?
3. **Review the blowdown**: is it continuous or intermittent? If continuous and
   >1%, consider water softening or heat recovery.
</Callout>
