What you collect, how often, and how you store it determines what insights you can get.

## Meter types & Collection frequency

**Cumulative meters** — manual reading monthly. **Interval data** (30-min) — ideal for M&T. **Real-time** — spot anomalies instantly.

For M&T, **interval data (30-min or hourly) is ideal**. Frequency matters:
- Monthly: rough trending only
- Daily: good baseline
- Hourly+: excellent, see daily cycles

**Practical:** Start with daily or 30-minute from smart meters or wireless submeters.

## Data storage & quality

Store in: spreadsheet (free but manual), utility portal (supplier access), data logger with cloud (£500–2,000), or BMS if available.

Before analysis, clean: fill small gaps, flag outliers, verify against utility bills.
