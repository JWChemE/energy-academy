A 10% increase might mean nothing if it was unusually cold. **Normalisation** adjusts for weather/occupancy so you compare apples-to-apples.

## Degree-days

**HDD** (heating degree-days) = max(0, 15.5°C − daily average temp)

Cold month (5°C avg): HDD = 10.5/day × 30 = 315 HDD.
Mild month (12°C avg): HDD = 3.5/day × 30 = 105 HDD.

Same building uses much more energy in the cold month. Normalising accounts for this.

Heating load ≈ baseline + sensitivity × HDD.

## Other normalisers

- Occupancy, production volume, hours of operation
- Cooling season uses **cooling degree-days (CDD)** = max(0, temp − 21°C)

Good M&T tracks these so you can decompose: "Energy up 5%, occupancy up 10%, so efficiency improved."
