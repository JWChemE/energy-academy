Monitoring & Targeting (M&T) is the discipline of using meter data to drive continuous energy improvement. Without it, you're flying blind.

## The power of data

Energy is invisible. You can't see 50 kW being wasted in a leaking steam trap or an oversized pump. But **the meter sees it**.

M&T turns that data into:
1. **A baseline** — your reference point for "no improvement"
2. **Targets** — ambitious goals for reduction
3. **Action** — identifying and fixing what's wrong
4. **Proof** — verifying savings are real (Measurement & Verification)

## The discipline

M&T is not a one-off audit. It's a continuous cycle: collect data, compare to baseline/targets, spot anomalies, investigate, fix, verify, repeat.

Buildings that do this routinely save 10–20% of energy compared to those that don't monitor.

## What data to collect?

Ideally: total electricity (kWh, kW, power factor), total gas, thermal systems (boiler, chiller, hot water), sub-systems (HVAC, lighting, major equipment), and external drivers (temperature, occupancy, production volume).

Even without sub-meters, total consumption + outdoor temperature gives you a baseline.

## Why barriers exist

Many buildings don't do M&T because: no baseline ("we don't know normal"), no tools (data isn't collected), no time (nobody owns it), no targets ("reduce energy" is vague).

Fixing these is straightforward and usually very low cost.
