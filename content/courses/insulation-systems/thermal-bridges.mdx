In an insulated building, the weak points are the bridges - places where heat sidesteps the insulation. Address the main areas and you address the bridges with them, but they deserve explicit attention.

## What a thermal bridge is

A **thermal bridge** is a localised area of higher heat flow, caused either by a more conductive material penetrating the insulation (a steel beam, a concrete balcony) or by geometry (an external corner, where more heat escapes than a flat area). Heat takes the path of least resistance, concentrating where insulation is interrupted.

## Why they matter in insulation work

Bridges can add 10-30% to the heat loss calculated from the plain element U-values if ignored. Worse, they create cold inner surfaces that:
- Collect condensation and grow mould
- Cause visible "ghosting" (dark staining tracking the cold studs or junctions)
- Chill occupants nearby

In retrofit especially, adding insulation to the main surfaces makes the untreated bridges relatively colder, sometimes turning a previously harmless junction into a condensation risk.

## Common bridge locations

- Window and door reveals, lintels, and sills
- Wall-to-floor and wall-to-roof junctions
- Balconies and canopies that penetrate the wall
- Steel or concrete structural elements crossing the insulation line
- Service penetrations and fixings

## Designing them out

- Keep the insulation layer continuous - overlap it around junctions.
- Use **thermal breaks**: insulated lintels, cavity closers, low-conductivity structural connectors at balconies.
- Insulate window reveals and carry wall insulation onto the frame.
- For solid-wall internal insulation, return the insulation a short distance onto party walls and around openings to lengthen the heat path.

## The temperature factor check

Designers verify junctions using the **temperature factor (fRsi)** - a measure of how warm the inner surface stays. Keeping fRsi above about 0.75 ensures the surface stays clear of the dew point, preventing mould. Accredited construction details provide pre-calculated, safe junctions - the simplest route to bridge-free insulation.
