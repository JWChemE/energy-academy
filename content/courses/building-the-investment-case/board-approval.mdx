The investment case culminates in a decision. Securing approval from the board or investment committee - the final hurdle - draws together everything in this course: a sound case, well-presented, with the relationships and strategy to win the vote.

## The approval moment

All the analysis, structuring, and presentation lead to a single moment: the decision-maker says yes or no. Understanding how approval actually happens - the process, the people, and the dynamics - lets the energy manager navigate it successfully rather than leaving it to chance.

## Understand the approval process

Approval follows a process that varies by organisation and project size:
- **Approval thresholds** - who can approve what (a manager, a director, the board, the investment committee)
- **The decision forum** - where and how decisions are made
- **The timetable** - when proposals are submitted and decided (often fixed cycles)
- **The criteria** - the financial and strategic tests applied
- **The paperwork** - the format and information required

Knowing the process - the thresholds, the forum, the timetable, the criteria - lets the energy manager prepare the right case, for the right people, at the right time, in the right form. Misjudging it wastes good proposals.

## Know the decision-makers

Approval is granted by people, and people differ (the stakeholder link):
- Who sits on the committee or board?
- What does each care about (cost, risk, carbon, reputation)?
- Who are the likely supporters, sceptics, and swing votes?
- Who has influence over the decision?

Understanding the decision-makers lets the energy manager tailor the case and the advocacy - addressing each one's priorities and concerns, and focusing effort on the swing votes who will decide the outcome.

## Build support beforehand

Approval is often won before the formal meeting, not in it (the influence link):
- **Pre-brief key decision-makers** - so the proposal is not a surprise, and concerns are addressed in advance
- **Secure a champion** - ideally a senior advocate who will support it in the room (the board-sponsor link)
- **Build the coalition** - line up the supporting functions (operations, finance, sustainability)
- **Address objections early** - resolve the blockers before the decision, not during it

A proposal that arrives at the committee with its concerns pre-addressed and its champions briefed is far more likely to pass than one presented cold. The groundwork of relationships and pre-engagement often decides the outcome.

## Anticipate and address objections

Every case faces objections. Anticipate them and prepare:
- **"We don't have the capital"** - offer alternative funding structures (the financing link)
- **"The savings are uncertain"** - present the verification commitment and conservative estimates (the M&V and savings links)
- **"It's risky"** - show the risk analysis and mitigation (the risk link)
- **"Why now / why this?"** - the cost of inaction and the strategic fit
- **"Other priorities compete"** - the portfolio and strategic case

Having credible, evidence-based responses ready - and pre-empting the major objections in the case - prevents the proposal being derailed by a question the energy manager should have foreseen.

## The role of track record

A powerful, often-underestimated factor is the energy manager's track record (the self-leadership and M&V links):
- Past projects that delivered the savings they promised
- A history of accurate, verified results
- Credibility built over time

A board that has seen previous energy projects deliver what was promised approves the next one far more readily. This is why the integrity of savings estimates, and the commitment to verify them, matter so much - they build the credibility that makes future approvals easier. Over time, a strong track record becomes the energy manager's most valuable asset in winning investment.

## After approval

Approval is a milestone, not the end:
- **Deliver the project** well, on budget and on time
- **Verify the savings** and report them honestly (the M&V link)
- **Communicate the success** - building credibility for the next case
- **Feed the result** into the track record and the case for further investment

Delivering and verifying approved projects is what sustains the cycle - each success making the next approval easier, building the momentum that funds an ambitious, ongoing energy programme.

## Bringing the course together

Securing approval draws together the whole course: a rigorous financial appraisal (NPV, IRR), honest risk assessment, the full value including co-benefits, a sound baseline and credible savings, a well-chosen funding structure, persuasive presentation, and strategic portfolio thinking - delivered to the right people, through the right process, with the relationships and track record to win the decision. Mastered together, these turn energy opportunities into funded projects - and a stream of funded projects into the transformation of an organisation's energy performance.
