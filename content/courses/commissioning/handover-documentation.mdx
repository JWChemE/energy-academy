**Handover documentation** is what's left behind after commissioning for the building operator.

## Essential documents

**1. Commissioning report:**
- Executive summary (key findings, defects found/fixed, energy baseline)
- Detailed test results (all systems, pass/fail, issues)
- As-built drawings (marked with actual installed layout, setpoints, configurations)
- Lessons learned

**2. Operation manuals:**
- One per major system (boiler, chiller, HVAC, BMS)
- How to start, stop, adjust setpoints
- Typical operation modes (heating season, cooling season, unoccupied)
- Emergency procedures (what to do if it fails)
- Contact info for service providers

**3. Maintenance schedule:**
- Annual tasks (filter changes, sensor calibration, cleaning)
- 5-year tasks (major servicing, replacement)
- Contact info for maintenance contractors

**4. Control documentation:**
- BMS user guide (how to log in, view data, change setpoints)
- Control sequences (pseudocode or flowcharts showing how each system responds)
- Alarm thresholds and what they mean

**5. Training sign-off:**
- Operator attended training: Yes/No, date
- Topics covered (how to operate, emergency procedures, efficiency tips)
- Operator signature confirming understanding

## Importance

Poor documentation leads to:
- Operators not knowing how to use systems (waste energy, comfort complaints)
- Maintenance contractors guessing at service procedures (poor upkeep)
- New building managers unable to troubleshoot (inefficiency persists)

Invest in clear, concise documentation. It pays dividends over the building's 20-30 year life.
