**ETICS** (Existing Building Commissioning through Targeted Improvements and Monitoring) is a framework for retrofitting and optimising existing buildings.

## Why existing buildings need it

Most existing buildings are not optimised:
- Controls have drifted post-commissioning (sensors no longer calibrated)
- Occupancy or use changed (building designed for 500 people, now 200)
- Equipment is oversized and runs inefficiently at part load
- Control sequences are incomplete or poorly tuned

ETICS provides a systematic approach to identify and fix these issues.

## ETICS phases

**Phase 1: Assessment**
- Energy audit (consumption, benchmarking)
- System walkthrough (age, condition, maintenance)
- Control and sensor review
- Identification of quick wins and capital projects

**Phase 2: Priority quick-win implementation**
- Low-cost, high-impact improvements (controls tuning, sensor calibration, thermostat adjustments)
- Payback typically < 1 year

**Phase 3: Capital projects**
- Equipment replacement, building envelope, renewable energy
- Longer payback (3–10 years)

**Phase 4: Ongoing commissioning**
- Continuous monitoring and annual re-commissioning
- Ensures performance persists

## Success factors

- Executive commitment (budget and authority)
- Designate an owner (energy manager or facilities director)
- Measure baseline before and after each project
- Regular (quarterly or annual) re-commissioning to prevent drift

ETICS projects typically save 10–30% of energy, with payback 2–5 years.
