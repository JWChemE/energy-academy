**Re-commissioning** is repeating key commissioning tasks to restore performance.

## When to re-commission

- After 1–2 years (detect early drift)
- After major equipment replacement or renovation
- After control system reprogramming
- If energy consumption unexpectedly rises
- Before major energy audit or investment decision

## Quick re-commissioning checklist

- **Sensors:** Calibrate key temperature, humidity, pressure sensors
- **Setpoints:** Verify thermostat setpoints unchanged from design
- **Sequences:** Walk through control logic; verify correct response
- **Equipment:** Check boiler/chiller efficiency; measure flow; listen for unusual noise
- **Maintenance:** Review maintenance logs; identify deferred items

Quick re-commissioning: 2–5 days, £2,000–5,000 cost.
Savings detected: Usually 5–10% energy reduction from correcting drift.
Payback: Often immediate (savings exceed cost in first year).

## Continuous commissioning

Rather than wait 1–2 years, monitor continuously:
- Daily: BMS trending (energy, setpoints, equipment runtime)
- Monthly: Review alarms and anomalies
- Quarterly: Sensor spot-checks
- Annually: Full re-commissioning

Continuous commissioning costs more upfront but catches issues immediately, preventing months of wasted energy.
