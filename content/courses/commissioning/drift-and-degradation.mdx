**Commissioning drift** is the gradual degradation of system performance after commissioning.

## Common causes

**Sensor drift:** Temperature sensors lose calibration over time (0.5–2% per year typical). After 2 years, a Pt100 sensor may be ±1–2°C off.

**Control tuning drift:** Operators manually adjust setpoints over time; original tuning is lost.

**Equipment fouling:** Heat exchangers scale, filters clog, coils get dusty. Efficiency drops.

**Maintenance neglect:** Belts loosen, bearings wear, refrigerant slowly leaks.

**Load changes:** Building occupancy increases, use pattern changes. Original control logic no longer optimal.

## Detection

Watch for:
- Rising energy consumption (heating or cooling) without occupancy/weather explanation
- Setpoint creep (thermostat slowly drifted from original)
- Comfort complaints (room too hot/cold)
- Sensor readings inconsistent with intuition (outdoor temp sensor on sunny wall reads 5°C high)

Trending data (from BMS) reveals these issues within weeks to months.

## Prevention

- Annual sensor recalibration
- Quarterly controls audit (check setpoints, verify sequences)
- Regular equipment maintenance (cleaning, bearing lubrication, filter changes)
- Annual re-commissioning (quick check of key parameters)

Cost to prevent: ~£500–1,500/year for a typical building.
Cost of not preventing: 5–15% energy waste = £5,000–50,000/year (depending on building size).
