## What these two cases test

**Case 1** is the wort heat-recovery opportunity named twice already in this module: a missed capture of hot cooling water running to drain. **Case 2** is a glycol cooling shortfall caused by a fouled condenser, the same fault mechanism the [refrigeration and heat pumps course](/courses/refrigeration-and-heat-pumps/practical-improvements) covers for any refrigeration plant, here costing a fermenter its target temperature during peak fermentation.

<Callout variant="key" title="Same method, brewery-specific numbers">
Both cases use exactly the calculation methods from this module (sensible heat from mass, specific heat and temperature change; capacity scaling with approach temperature) applied to fresh, brewery-specific data. If you can work through the [wort heat-recovery lesson](/courses/breweries/heat-recovery-in-the-brewhouse) and the [refrigeration lesson](/courses/breweries/refrigeration-and-glycol-optimisation) unaided, these two should feel like direct applications, not new material.
</Callout>
