## How the stages work

This capstone follows the same staged format as the [Energy Audits course's own capstone](/courses/energy-audits/field-audit-capstone), because a brewery audit *is* an energy audit: this course has given you everything sector-specific on top of that general method:

1. **Plan & Scope**: decide the audit level, draw the boundary correctly (does an off-site maltster count?), and flag brewery-specific site hazards before you walk in.
2. **On-Site**: the walkthrough observations and instrumentation choices that matter in a brewhouse and cellar, plus twelve months of real production and utility data with one figure missing for you to derive.
3. **Analyse & Normalise**: build the multi-variable model relating electricity to both production and cooling degree-days (the brewery equivalent of the weather-normalisation method from measurement & verification), then compute Fenmarsh's actual thermal and electrical intensity.
4. **Opportunities**: quantify the wort heat-recovery project and a glycol setpoint change, then decide what actually belongs in the final recommendations (and what doesn't, because it would cross a food-safety line).
5. **Report & M&V**: what a credible brewery audit report must contain, and how its savings should actually be verified a year later.

<Callout variant="key" title="The normalisation is the hard part, and the point">
Stage 3's regression, isolating a production coefficient, then a cooling-degree-day coefficient, then using both to back-solve a missing month's data, is deliberately the most demanding calculation in this capstone. It's also the single skill that turns "our bills went up" into a defensible, quantified finding a client can act on. Everything in stage 4 depends on getting stage 3 right.
</Callout>

## What "audit-ready" looks like here

A strong run through this capstone means every stage's answers hold up on their own: the boundary decision defensible, the hazards genuinely brewery-specific, the regression exact, the opportunities correctly separated from the food-safety floor, and the final report grounded in a normalised baseline rather than a raw year-on-year bill comparison. That's the same bar the [Energy Audits capstone](/courses/energy-audits/field-audit-capstone) sets for Frostfield Foods: this is that same standard, applied to a brewery's own processes, benchmarks and regulation.
