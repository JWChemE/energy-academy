Beyond temperature, modern buildings need humidity, pressure, and flow measurement.

## Humidity (RH - Relative Humidity)

**Sensors:** Capacitive (most common), resistive, or psychrometric.

Pros: Capacitive sensors are cheap and reasonably accurate (±2–5%).
Cons: Drift over time, affected by temperature, condensation kills them.

Applications: HVAC latent load control, datacenter humidity management, laboratory environments.

Placement: Away from supply diffusers, not in condensation zones.

## Pressure sensors

**Applications:** Duct static pressure (fan speed control), chiller pressure (flow), pump pressure (system monitoring).

Types: Diaphragm (most common), capacitive, strain gauge.

Typical accuracy: ±1–2% of full scale.

Energy impact: Low duct static pressure = fans can run slower (VFD on fan) = big energy savings.

## Flow sensors

**Types:** Turbine (mechanical rotation counted), magnetic (Coriolis, very accurate), ultrasonic (non-invasive).

Applications: Chilled water flow (chiller control), hot water flow (boiler modulation), air flow (VAV box damper control).

Challenge: Pipe scale and debris plug mechanical sensors. Ultrasonic avoids this.

## Integration errors

A poorly placed or uncalibrated sensor ruins control. Examples:

- Humidity sensor in condensation zone reads 100% year-round; dehumidification runs 24/7 (waste).
- Flow sensor reading 50% of actual flow → pump runs 2× harder to compensate (energy waste).

Budget for sensor maintenance and annual recalibration.
