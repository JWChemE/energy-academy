A **Building Management System (BMS)** centralises control of all building systems: HVAC, lighting, power, security, fire.

## Centralised vs distributed

**Centralised:** All control logic in one computer (traditional). Pros: one place to manage everything. Cons: single point of failure.

**Distributed:** Intelligence spread across local controllers (VAV controllers, chiller controller, etc.) talking to a central BMS. Pros: more robust, faster local response. Cons: complex integration.

**Modern approach:** Distributed with central oversight — each system has a controller, but BMS monitors and coordinates.

## Network protocols

**BACnet:** Open standard, widely supported, popular in building automation.

**Modbus:** Simpler, older, still widely used.

**LonWorks:** Proprietary but powerful, common in larger systems.

**Proprietary:** Manufacturers lock systems (e.g., Honeywell, Johnson Controls). Integration is harder.

**Trend:** Cloud-based systems (Amazon, Microsoft) growing in adoption for data analysis and remote management.

## Data points

A typical building BMS logs thousands of points:
- Room temperatures (hundreds)
- Damper positions, valve positions
- Equipment runtimes, alarms
- Energy consumption (kWh)

Data archival: 1–5 years typical; longer for compliance (utility contracts, audits).
