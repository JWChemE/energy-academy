**Multi-loop control** coordinates multiple control loops working together (e.g., chiller, boiler, pump speed, damper position).

## Cascade control

Inner loop: Controls fast-responding variable (valve position, damper angle, pump speed).
Outer loop: Controls slower-responding variable (room temperature, supply water temp, pressure).

Example: BMS sets heating supply water setpoint (60°C). Lower-level loop controls valve to maintain 60°C. This is faster and more stable than BMS directly controlling valve.

## Precedent hierarchy

When multiple control loops conflict, a hierarchy determines priority.

Example: Building with cooling and heating:
1. **Highest priority:** Safety (prevent freeze-up, overpressure)
2. **Next:** Comfort (maintain setpoint)
3. **Next:** Energy efficiency (minimize consumption)
4. **Lowest:** Convenience (nice-to-have features)

If cooling and heating both demand action, comfort (temperature) overrides energy efficiency.

## Coordination

Loops must not fight each other.

Bad: Heating loop opens valve; cooling loop simultaneously opens chiller. Result: simultaneous heating and cooling (waste).

Good: Heating disabled when cooling is active. Or, use a setpoint "deadband": heating at 18°C, cooling only if temp > 24°C. In between, neither acts.

## Practical benefit

Well-coordinated multi-loop control can save 10–20% energy compared to independent, poorly coordinated loops.
