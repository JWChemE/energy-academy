## What these two cases test

**Case 1** is the silent drift the [heat-recovery lesson](/courses/dairies/heat-recovery-in-dairies) warned about: a pasteuriser whose regeneration has slipped from 92% to 84%, with the hot section absorbing the difference and no alarm anywhere, because the milk still reaches its validated temperature. **Case 2** is the [ice bank](/courses/dairies/ice-banks-and-load-shifting) after a controls upgrade quietly moved its charging into the day rate: identical ice, £35,000 a year dearer, and invisible because the ice is always ready.

<Callout variant="key" title="Same method, dairy numbers">
Both cases use only arithmetic you already have: mass × specific heat × ΔT and an effectiveness share for the first; energy ÷ COP × tariff for the second. What they add is the sector's characteristic judgement: in both plants nothing is broken, both wrong answers on offer involve buying bigger machines, and both right answers are maintenance and a schedule, verified afterwards by a trend someone now owns.
</Callout>
