A management system that is never checked drifts and decays. Internal audits are how an organisation verifies that its energy management system is working as intended, finds where it is not, and drives the corrections that keep it effective.

## What internal auditing is

An **internal audit** is a systematic, independent check of the energy management system against the requirements of ISO 50001 and the organisation's own procedures. It asks: is the system actually doing what it is supposed to do? It is the formal core of the "Check" stage of PDCA.

Internal means conducted by the organisation itself (by trained internal auditors, or by external help acting on its behalf), as distinct from the external certification audit. It is the organisation checking itself, honestly, to find and fix its own gaps before they undermine performance - or before an external auditor finds them.

## What audits examine

An internal audit checks both **conformance** and **effectiveness**:
- **Conformance:** does the system meet the requirements? Is there a policy, are there EnPIs, are SEUs identified, are action plans in place, is monitoring happening, are records kept?
- **Effectiveness:** is the system actually improving energy performance, or just generating paperwork? Are targets being met? Are actions delivering savings?

The second question matters most. A system can tick every box yet fail to improve energy performance - and a good audit probes for real results, not just documentation.

## How audits work

A typical internal audit:
1. **Plans** the scope - which parts of the system, which sites, against which requirements
2. **Gathers evidence** - reviewing documents and records, interviewing people, observing operations, checking data
3. **Compares** the evidence against the requirements and intended performance
4. **Identifies findings** - conformities, nonconformities, and opportunities for improvement
5. **Reports** the findings to management
6. **Tracks** the resulting corrective actions to completion

Auditors must be **independent** of the area they audit - you cannot objectively audit your own work - which is why organisations train several auditors who check each other's areas.

## Nonconformities and corrective action

When an audit finds a **nonconformity** - a failure to meet a requirement or a gap in effectiveness - it triggers **corrective action**:
- **Identify the nonconformity** clearly
- **Investigate the root cause** - why did it happen? (not just the symptom)
- **Correct it** - fix the immediate problem
- **Address the cause** - prevent recurrence
- **Verify** the correction worked

The discipline of root-cause analysis is key: fixing the symptom while leaving the cause means the problem returns. Good corrective action asks *why* the system failed and fixes that.

## Audits as improvement, not blame

The spirit of internal auditing is improvement, not punishment. Audits should be:
- **Constructive** - finding opportunities to improve, not hunting for people to blame
- **Honest** - genuinely seeking gaps, not papering over them to look good
- **Valued** - seen as helping the organisation improve, so people engage openly

An audit culture of blame drives problems underground; a culture of honest improvement surfaces them where they can be fixed. The energy leader sets this tone (the leadership link).

## Feeding the system

Internal audit findings feed directly into:
- **Corrective actions** that fix the gaps
- **Management review** (the next lesson), where senior leaders consider the audit results
- **Continual improvement** of both energy performance and the system itself

Conducted regularly and honestly, internal audits are the immune system of the EnMS - continually detecting and correcting the drift, gaps, and weaknesses that would otherwise let the system decay. They keep the management system alive, effective, and genuinely improving energy performance, year after year - and prepare the organisation to pass external certification with confidence.
