ISO 50001 is the international standard for energy management systems. It gives organisations a structured, recognised framework for managing energy systematically - turning ad-hoc efforts into a disciplined, continually improving process.

## What ISO 50001 is

ISO 50001 is a voluntary international standard that specifies the requirements for an **energy management system (EnMS)**. It does not tell you what technologies to install or what targets to hit; it sets out *how* to manage energy - the structure, processes, and discipline that drive continual improvement in energy performance.

Think of it as a management framework, not a technical manual. It works for any organisation, of any size, in any sector.

## The Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle

At its heart, ISO 50001 follows the **Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)** cycle - the engine of continual improvement common to all ISO management standards:

- **Plan:** understand your energy use, establish a baseline and performance indicators, set objectives and targets, and make action plans
- **Do:** implement the action plans and operate the system
- **Check:** monitor and measure performance against the baseline and targets, and audit the system
- **Act:** review results at management level and act to improve - then the cycle repeats

This loop ensures energy management is not a one-off project but a continuously turning wheel of improvement.

## Key requirements

ISO 50001 requires an organisation to:
- Establish an **energy policy** committing to improvement (the subject of its own course)
- Identify **significant energy uses (SEUs)** - where most energy goes
- Establish an **energy baseline** and **energy performance indicators (EnPIs)**
- Set **objectives, targets, and action plans**
- Ensure **competence, awareness, and communication**
- **Monitor, measure, and analyse** energy performance
- Conduct **internal audits** and **management reviews**
- Drive **continual improvement**

The rest of this course works through these in turn.

## Why adopt it

Organisations adopt ISO 50001 for several reasons:
- **Systematic results:** the structure delivers more, and more durable, savings than ad-hoc efforts - typically continual year-on-year improvement
- **Credibility:** certification demonstrates commitment to stakeholders, customers, and regulators
- **Compliance:** in some jurisdictions it satisfies, or eases, mandatory energy schemes (in the UK it can route compliance with ESOS)
- **Embedding:** it institutionalises energy management, surviving staff changes (the governance link)
- **Cost:** systematic energy management reliably cuts energy costs

## Certification versus conformance

An organisation can:
- **Certify** to ISO 50001 - an external body audits and formally certifies conformance (valuable for credibility and compliance)
- **Conform** without certifying - use the framework to manage energy well, without the cost and formality of certification

Many organisations adopt the structure for its benefits without seeking formal certification; others need the certificate for compliance or stakeholder reasons. The framework delivers value either way.
